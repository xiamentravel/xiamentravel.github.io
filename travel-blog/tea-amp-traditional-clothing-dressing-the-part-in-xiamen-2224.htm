Tea & Traditional Clothing: Dressing the Part in Xiamen

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Xiamen, a coastal gem in Fujian Province, is a city where tradition and modernity blend seamlessly. Beyond its famous Gulangyu Island and bustling Zhongshan Road, Xiamen offers a unique cultural experience centered around tea and traditional clothing. For travelers looking to immerse themselves in local customs, sipping tea while dressed in elegant Hanfu or Qipao is a must-try activity. Here’s how you can embrace this delightful fusion of flavors and fashion.

The Art of Tea in Xiamen

Tea culture runs deep in Xiamen, thanks to its proximity to Fujian’s renowned tea-producing regions like Anxi (home of Tieguanyin) and Wuyi Mountain (famous for Da Hong Pao). Whether you’re a tea connoisseur or a curious beginner, Xiamen’s teahouses and tea ceremonies offer a serene escape from the city’s hustle.

Where to Experience Authentic Tea Culture

  1. Tianfu Tea House – Nestled in the heart of Xiamen, this teahouse is a haven for tea lovers. Their traditional Gongfu tea ceremony showcases the meticulous process of brewing Fujian’s finest teas.

  2. Gulangyu’s Hidden Tea Gardens – Stroll through the island’s quieter lanes, and you’ll stumble upon quaint tea shops where owners gladly share stories behind each brew.

  3. Wuyi Tea Market – For those who want to take home a piece of Xiamen’s tea culture, this market offers a wide selection of loose-leaf teas, teaware, and even tea-infused snacks.

Tea Pairings with Local Snacks

No tea session is complete without traditional Fujian snacks. Try:
- Oolong Tea with Pineapple Cakes – The floral notes of Tieguanyin complement the buttery sweetness of these pastries.
- Jasmine Tea with Peanut Soup – A light, fragrant tea balances the richness of this local favorite.

Traditional Clothing: A Fashionable Journey Through Time

While tea engages the senses, traditional clothing adds a visual layer to Xiamen’s cultural tapestry. Hanfu (ancient Chinese attire) and Qipao (the iconic cheongsam) are making a comeback, especially among younger generations and tourists eager to connect with history.

Where to Rent or Buy Traditional Outfits

  1. Hanfu Studios in Zhongshan Road – Several boutiques offer rentals for photoshoots or leisurely walks through historic districts. Staff often help with styling, ensuring an authentic look.

  2. Qipao Tailors in Siming District – For a personalized touch, visit a tailor who can craft a custom Qipao in just a few days. Choose from classic silk or modern fabrics with Xiamen-inspired prints.

  3. Cultural Workshops – Some teahouses and cultural centers host events where you can learn to wear Hanfu properly while enjoying tea.

Best Photo Spots in Traditional Attire

  • Shuzhuang Garden (Gulangyu) – The classical Chinese garden with its pavilions and lotus ponds is a dreamy backdrop.
  • Hulishan Fortress – Contrast the elegance of Hanfu with the rugged coastal scenery.
  • Nanputuo Temple – The serene Buddhist temple adds a spiritual touch to your photos.

Combining Tea and Fashion: Unique Experiences

For the ultimate cultural immersion, try these activities:

Tea Ceremonies in Hanfu

Join a workshop where you’ll dress in traditional attire before learning the art of tea preparation. The slow, deliberate movements of the ceremony feel even more meaningful when you’re wearing flowing Hanfu sleeves.

Sunset Tea by the Beach

Head to Xiamen’s less crowded beaches like Huangcuo or Zengcuoan, where you can sip tea in a Qipao as the sun dips below the horizon. It’s a perfect blend of relaxation and sophistication.

Night Markets in Style

Zengcuoan’s night market isn’t just for street food—it’s also a great place to showcase your traditional outfit. Pair your look with a cup of milk tea (yes, even modern twists have their place!).

Tips for First-Timers

  • Fabric Matters – Xiamen’s humidity calls for lightweight materials like linen or breathable silk.
  • Accessorize Thoughtfully – Hairpins, folding fans, and embroidered pouches elevate the ensemble.
  • Respect the Culture – If you’re unsure about styling, ask locals or workshop guides for advice.

Xiamen’s tea and traditional clothing scene is more than a trend—it’s a celebration of heritage. Whether you’re posing in a Qipao by the sea or savoring Tieguanyin in a tranquil teahouse, these experiences will leave you with unforgettable memories.
