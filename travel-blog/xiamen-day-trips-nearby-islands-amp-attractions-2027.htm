Xiamen Day Trips: Nearby Islands & Attractions

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Xiamen, a coastal city in Fujian Province, is a gem of Southern China known for its laid-back vibe, colonial architecture, and stunning island getaways. While the city itself offers plenty to explore, the real magic lies just offshore—where turquoise waters, sandy beaches, and lush greenery create the perfect escape. Whether you're a history buff, a beach lover, or a foodie, Xiamen’s nearby islands and attractions promise unforgettable day trips.

Must-Visit Islands Near Xiamen

Gulangyu Island: The Piano Isle

No trip to Xiamen is complete without visiting Gulangyu Island, a UNESCO World Heritage Site just a 10-minute ferry ride from the city. This car-free island is famous for its colonial-era villas, winding cobblestone streets, and rich musical heritage—earning it the nickname "Piano Island."

Top Things to Do on Gulangyu:

  • Shuzhuang Garden: A seaside garden with hidden caves, elegant bridges, and the iconic Piano Museum.
  • Sunlight Rock: Hike up for panoramic views of Xiamen’s skyline and the surrounding ocean.
  • Longtou Road: Stroll through this bustling street lined with souvenir shops, cafés, and Fujian-style snacks like shacha noodles and oyster omelets.

Pro tip: Visit early in the morning or late afternoon to avoid crowds.

Wuyi Mountain: A Nature Lover’s Paradise

Though not an island, Wuyi Mountain (about 3 hours from Xiamen) is worth the detour for its dramatic cliffs, emerald rivers, and ancient tea plantations. This UNESCO-listed site is perfect for hiking, bamboo rafting, and sipping on Da Hong Pao tea, one of China’s most prized oolongs.

Kinmen Islands: A Slice of Taiwan

Just a 30-minute ferry from Xiamen, the Kinmen Islands (administered by Taiwan) offer a unique blend of Chinese and Taiwanese culture. Highlights include:
- Qionglin Village: A well-preserved Fujianese settlement with traditional stone houses.
- Kinmen Kaoliang Liquor Factory: Sample the island’s signature sorghum liquor.
- Zhaishan Tunnel: A historic military tunnel from the Cold War era.

Note: Bring your passport, as Kinmen requires a Taiwan entry permit.

Hidden Gems & Off-the-Beaten-Path Spots

Tianzhu Mountain: A Spiritual Retreat

For a quieter alternative to Gulangyu, head to Tianzhu Mountain on the mainland. This scenic area features Buddhist temples, hiking trails, and the towering Tianzhu Rock. Don’t miss the chance to try vegetarian Buddhist cuisine at the mountain’s monasteries.

Huandao Road: Xiamen’s Coastal Playground

Rent a bike and cruise along Huandao Road, a 43-kilometer loop that skirts Xiamen’s coastline. Stop at:
- Xiamen University: One of China’s most beautiful campuses, with palm-lined lanes and ocean views.
- Hulishan Fortress: A 19th-century coastal defense site with massive Krupp cannons.
- Baicheng Beach: A popular spot for sunset picnics and seaside cafés.

Foodie Adventures Near Xiamen

Zhangzhou: The Birthplace of Minnan Cuisine

A short drive from Xiamen, Zhangzhou is a food haven. Must-tries include:
- Zhangzhou noodles: Springy wheat noodles in a savory pork broth.
- Tianjinluo (snail noodles): A local delicacy made with river snails and rice noodles.
- Salt-baked chicken: Tender chicken slow-cooked in a salt crust.

Tong’an District: Seafood Galore

Tong’an’s fishing villages serve the freshest seafood in the region. Visit Deng’ao Fishing Port for a seafood market experience, or dine at a waterfront restaurant for dishes like steamed grouper and stir-fried clams with garlic.

Practical Tips for Day Trips from Xiamen

  • Ferries to Gulangyu: Book tickets online in advance (especially on weekends).
  • Weather: Avoid typhoon season (July–September); spring and autumn are ideal.
  • Transport: Use Didi (China’s Uber) or public buses for mainland trips.
  • Language: Learn basic Mandarin phrases or use a translation app—English isn’t widely spoken outside tourist areas.

From cultural landmarks to culinary delights, Xiamen’s nearby islands and attractions offer endless opportunities for exploration. Pack your day bag, grab your camera, and get ready to discover the best of Southern China—one island at a time.
