A Guide to Xiamen’s Famous Huandao Road

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Xiamen’s Huandao Road, also known as the Island Ring Road, is one of the most scenic coastal routes in China. Stretching approximately 43 kilometers, this picturesque road offers breathtaking views of the ocean, lush greenery, and a mix of urban and natural landscapes. Whether you're a cyclist, a jogger, or simply someone who enjoys leisurely strolls, Huandao Road has something for everyone.

Why Huandao Road is a Must-Visit

Stunning Coastal Views

One of the biggest draws of Huandao Road is its uninterrupted views of the sea. The road winds along Xiamen’s coastline, offering glimpses of the turquoise waters of the Taiwan Strait. On clear days, you can even see the distant outline of Kinmen Island. The best spots for photography include Hulishan Fortress and Zengcuoan Beach, where the sunsets are nothing short of magical.

Perfect for Outdoor Activities

Huandao Road is a paradise for fitness enthusiasts. The well-paved paths are ideal for:
- Cycling: Rent a bike from one of the many rental shops and enjoy a smooth ride with ocean breezes.
- Jogging: Locals and tourists alike flock here for morning and evening runs.
- Walking: Take a leisurely walk and soak in the serene atmosphere.

Cultural and Historical Landmarks

While the natural beauty is a highlight, Huandao Road also boasts several cultural and historical sites:
- Xiamen University: One of China’s most beautiful campuses, located near the southern section of the road.
- Nanputuo Temple: A serene Buddhist temple nestled at the foot of Wulao Peak.
- Hulishan Fortress: A historic coastal defense site with well-preserved cannons.

Best Sections of Huandao Road to Explore

The Southern Stretch (Xiamen University to Zengcuoan)

This section is lively and bustling, passing by Xiamen University and the popular Bai Cheng Beach. The area is packed with cafes, street food vendors, and boutique shops, making it a great place to take a break.

The Eastern Coastline (Hulishan to Huangcuo)

Here, the road becomes quieter, with more focus on nature. The Music Square and Huangcuo Beach are perfect spots to relax. This section is especially popular among couples and photographers.

The Northern Loop (Wuyuanwan to Xianyue Road)

If you prefer a more secluded experience, the northern part of Huandao Road offers tranquil beaches and mangrove forests. The Wuyuanwan Wetland Park is a hidden gem for birdwatching.

Tips for Visiting Huandao Road

Best Time to Go

  • Early Morning: Beat the crowds and enjoy the cool breeze.
  • Late Afternoon: Ideal for sunset views and pleasant temperatures.
    Avoid midday in summer, as the heat can be intense.

How to Get Around

  • Bike Rentals: Affordable and convenient, with stations along the route.
  • Electric Scooters: A faster option for covering longer distances.
  • Walking: Best for short sections if you want to take your time.

Where to Eat

Huandao Road is lined with eateries offering fresh seafood and local Fujian cuisine. Don’t miss:
- Zengcuoan’s seafood stalls
- Cafes near Xiamen University
- Street food vendors along the boardwalk

Hidden Gems Along Huandao Road

The Secret Beaches

While Bai Cheng Beach and Zengcuoan Beach are well-known, smaller, less crowded beaches like Guyong Beach offer a more private experience.

Art and Culture Spots

  • Zengcuoan Art Zone: A hub for local artists and creatives.
  • Music Square: Occasionally hosts live performances.

Scenic Parks

  • Wuyuanwan Wetland Park: A peaceful retreat with walking trails.
  • Hulishan Park: Combines history with stunning views.

Final Thoughts

Huandao Road is more than just a road—it’s an experience. Whether you're looking for adventure, relaxation, or cultural enrichment, this iconic route delivers. Pack your sunscreen, grab a bike, and get ready to explore one of Xiamen’s most beloved attractions.
