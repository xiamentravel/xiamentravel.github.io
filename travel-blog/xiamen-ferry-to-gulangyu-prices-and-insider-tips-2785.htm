Xiamen Ferry to Gulangyu: Prices and Insider Tips

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Gulangyu, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is one of China’s most charming island destinations. Just a short ferry ride from Xiamen, this car-free island is famous for its colonial architecture, winding lanes, and vibrant arts scene. But before you set foot on this enchanting island, you’ll need to navigate the ferry system—something many travelers find confusing.

Here’s everything you need to know about taking the Xiamen ferry to Gulangyu, including ticket prices, departure points, and insider tips to make your journey smooth and enjoyable.

Ferry Routes and Departure Points

There are two main ferry terminals in Xiamen that service Gulangyu:

Xiamen International Cruise Center (Dongdu Pier)

This is the primary departure point for most tourists. Located near Xiamen’s city center, it offers frequent departures to Gulangyu’s Sanqiutian Pier (for daytime trips) and Nei Cuo Pier (for evening returns).

Lun Du Ferry Terminal

This terminal is closer to downtown Xiamen but mainly serves local residents. Foreign tourists are technically allowed to use it, but tickets are harder to secure due to limited availability.

Ferry Ticket Prices

Ferry tickets to Gulangyu come in different tiers, depending on seating and route:

Standard Round-Trip Tickets

  • Economy Class (No seating assignment) – ¥35
  • Deluxe Class (Assigned seating, air-conditioned cabin) – ¥50
  • VIP Cabin (Premium seating, better views) – ¥100+

Night Cruise Tickets

If you’re planning a sunset or evening trip, special night ferries run after 6 PM, usually priced at ¥50-80 for a round trip.

Important Notes on Tickets

  • Advance booking is mandatory – Tickets often sell out, especially on weekends and holidays.
  • Foreign passport holders must book through official channels – Some third-party sites don’t support non-Chinese IDs.
  • Same-day tickets are rare – Plan at least 1-2 days ahead.

How to Book Ferry Tickets

Official WeChat Mini-Program

The easiest way to book is via the "厦门轮渡+" (Xiamen Ferry+) WeChat mini-program. You’ll need a Chinese phone number for verification.

Ctrip or Trip.com

International travelers can use these platforms, but availability may be limited.

At the Terminal (Risky!)

If you’re feeling lucky, you can try buying tickets at the pier, but expect long lines and possible sell-outs.

Insider Tips for a Smooth Ferry Experience

Arrive Early, Especially on Weekends

Even with a pre-booked ticket, security checks and boarding can take time. Aim to arrive at least 30 minutes before departure.

Choose the Right Pier on Gulangyu

  • Sanqiutian Pier – Best for first-time visitors, close to major attractions like Shuzhuang Garden.
  • Nei Cuo Pier – Quieter, better for sunset views and boutique hotels.

Avoid Peak Travel Times

Chinese national holidays (October Golden Week, Spring Festival) mean massive crowds. If possible, visit on a weekday.

Bring Your Passport!

Foreign travelers must show their passport at check-in—no exceptions.

Consider a Guided Tour

If booking tickets seems overwhelming, some tours include ferry tickets and skip-the-line access.

Exploring Gulangyu: What to Do After the Ferry

Once you’ve arrived, the real adventure begins. Here are some must-see spots:

Colonial Architecture & Museums

  • Shuzhuang Garden – A seaside villa with stunning views.
  • Gulangyu Organ Museum – Home to rare antique organs.

Hidden Cafés & Street Food

  • Zhang Sanfeng Oyster Omelette – A local favorite.
  • Coffee shops with rooftop views – Perfect for a mid-day break.

Sunset at Sunlight Rock

Hike up for panoramic views of Xiamen’s skyline.

Final Thoughts

Taking the ferry to Gulangyu is the first step in an unforgettable journey. With the right planning, you can avoid the crowds, secure the best tickets, and make the most of your time on this magical island. Whether you're a history buff, a foodie, or just looking for a scenic escape, Gulangyu has something for everyone.

Now, all that’s left is to board the ferry and let the island’s charm sweep you away!
