Top 10 Rainy Day Activities in Xiamen

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Rainy days in Xiamen don’t have to put a damper on your travel plans. This coastal city is packed with indoor and covered attractions that make it a perfect destination even when the weather isn’t cooperating. From cultural hotspots to cozy cafes, here are the top 10 rainy day activities to enjoy in Xiamen.

1. Explore Gulangyu Island’s Museums

Piano Museum

Gulangyu is known as "Piano Island," and the Piano Museum is a must-visit. Housed in a beautiful colonial villa, it showcases antique pianos from around the world. The melodic ambiance makes it a serene escape from the rain.

Shuzhuang Garden & Organ Museum

Adjacent to the Piano Museum, Shuzhuang Garden features stunning landscapes and the Organ Museum, where you can admire intricate pipe organs. The garden’s covered walkways allow you to enjoy the scenery without getting wet.

2. Sip Coffee in a Hidden Café

Xiamen’s café culture thrives in its narrow alleyways. Head to 32/HOW, a trendy spot with artisanal brews, or Thank You Café, known for its cozy vibe and creative drinks. These cafes often feature local art, making them perfect for a relaxed afternoon.

3. Visit Nanputuo Temple

This ancient Buddhist temple is a peaceful retreat. Wander through its grand halls, admire the intricate carvings, and enjoy the misty mountain views. The temple’s covered corridors provide shelter while you explore.

4. Shop at Zhongshan Road Pedestrian Street

Xiamen’s most famous shopping street is lined with arcades, so you can browse without worrying about the rain. From local snacks like peanut soup (花生汤) to quirky souvenirs, there’s plenty to discover.

5. Dive into Xiamen’s Art Scene at Red Town Art District

This creative hub is home to galleries, studios, and quirky installations. The Xiamen Art Museum here often hosts contemporary exhibitions. The industrial-chic vibe makes it a great spot for photos, even on gloomy days.

6. Relax at a Luxury Spa

Treat yourself at Mandara Spa (in the Pan Pacific Xiamen) or a local foot massage parlor. Rainy days are perfect for unwinding with a traditional Chinese massage or a rejuvenating facial.

7. Discover Xiamen’s History at the Overseas Chinese Museum

Learn about the diaspora of Fujianese people through fascinating exhibits. The museum’s spacious halls are ideal for a leisurely visit, and the artifacts provide deep insights into the region’s cultural heritage.

8. Take a Cooking Class

Why not learn to make Fujianese dishes like Buddha Jumps Over the Wall (佛跳墙) or oyster omelets (蚵仔煎)? Many local cooking schools offer hands-on classes—a fun way to spend a rainy afternoon.

9. Catch a Show at Xiamen International Conference Center

Check the schedule for concerts, theater performances, or cultural shows. The modern venue hosts everything from classical music to contemporary dance, ensuring an entertaining evening.

10. Play at Xiamen Science and Technology Museum

Great for families or curious adults, this interactive museum features exhibits on robotics, space, and more. The 4D cinema and VR experiences are especially thrilling when you’re avoiding the rain.

Rain or shine, Xiamen offers endless ways to enjoy your trip. Whether you’re indulging in local flavors, diving into history, or simply relaxing with a book in a charming café, these activities prove that bad weather can’t stop the fun.
