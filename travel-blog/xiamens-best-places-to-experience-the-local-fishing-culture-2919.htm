Xiamen’s Best Places to Experience the Local Fishing Culture

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Xiamen, a coastal city in Fujian Province, is a paradise for travelers seeking an authentic fishing culture experience. From bustling fish markets to serene fishing villages, the city offers a unique blend of tradition and modernity. Whether you’re a foodie, a history buff, or just someone who loves the sea, Xiamen’s fishing culture has something for everyone. Here are the top spots to immerse yourself in this vibrant way of life.

1. Shapowei Fishing Village: A Glimpse into the Past

Once a thriving fishing community, Shapowei has transformed into a trendy artsy district while still retaining its maritime roots. Stroll along the narrow alleys lined with old stone houses, and you’ll find remnants of its fishing heritage.

What to Do Here:

  • Visit the Shapowei Art Zone – Many old fishing huts have been converted into quirky cafes and galleries.
  • Try Fresh Seafood – Small family-run eateries serve dishes made from the day’s catch.
  • Photograph the Fishing Boats – The harbor is still active, with colorful wooden boats bobbing in the water.

2. Gaoqi Fishing Port: A Bustling Seafood Hub

If you want to see where Xiamen’s seafood comes from, Gaoqi Fishing Port is the place to go. This is one of the largest fishing ports in the city, where fishermen unload their hauls early in the morning.

Highlights of Gaoqi Fishing Port:

  • Morning Fish Market – Arrive before sunrise to witness the lively auction of fresh seafood.
  • Local Eateries – Nearby restaurants serve dishes like jianbing (seafood pancakes) and steamed fish.
  • Fishermen’s Stories – Chat with the locals to learn about their daily lives and traditions.

3. Gulangyu Island: A Historic Fishing Legacy

Gulangyu, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is famous for its colonial architecture, but few realize its deep connection to fishing culture. The island was once a key trading post for fishermen.

Must-See Spots on Gulangyu:

  • Haoyue Garden – Offers stunning views of the sea and remnants of old fishing docks.
  • Local Seafood Street – Try shacha noodles with fresh clams or grilled squid.
  • Fishermen’s Wharf – A quiet spot where you can watch small fishing boats come and go.

4. Zengcuoan Fishing Village: Where Tradition Meets Tourism

Zengcuoan, known for its hipster vibe, was originally a fishing village. Today, it’s a mix of old and new, with seafood stalls standing alongside boutique hotels.

Why Visit Zengcuoan?

  • Night Market – Sample street food like oyster omelets and fish ball soup.
  • Fishing Workshops – Some guesthouses offer hands-on experiences like net-mending.
  • Beachside Cafes – Sip coffee while watching fishermen haul in their nets.

5. Xiamen Seafood Market: A Food Lover’s Dream

No trip to Xiamen is complete without visiting its seafood markets. The most famous one is near the Xiamen University area, where vendors sell everything from live crabs to dried squid.

Tips for Visiting the Market:

  • Bargain Politely – Haggling is common, but keep it friendly.
  • Try Street Food – Don’t miss jian dui (fried sesame balls) or fish tofu.
  • Watch the Preparation – Some stalls will cook your seafood right in front of you.

6. Wuyuanwan: A Scenic Fishing Experience

For a more relaxed vibe, head to Wuyuanwan, a coastal area where fishing is still a way of life. The mangrove forests and calm waters make it a picturesque spot.

Activities in Wuyuanwan:

  • Boat Tours – Local fishermen offer short rides to see traditional fishing methods.
  • Bird Watching – The mangroves attract migratory birds, adding to the serene atmosphere.
  • Seafood BBQs – Some tours include fresh seafood grilled on the boat.

7. Hulishan Fortress: A Military Site with Fishing Roots

While primarily a historical military site, Hulishan Fortress overlooks the sea and offers insights into how fishing communities interacted with coastal defenses.

What Makes It Special?

  • Panoramic Ocean Views – Perfect for photography enthusiasts.
  • Nearby Fishing Spots – Locals still fish along the rocky shores.
  • Cultural Exhibits – Learn about Xiamen’s maritime history.

8. Jimei Fishing Village: A Hidden Gem

Jimei, often overshadowed by Xiamen’s more famous attractions, is a quiet fishing village with a strong sense of community.

Reasons to Explore Jimei:

  • Authentic Atmosphere – Fewer tourists mean a more genuine experience.
  • Turtleback Rock – A scenic spot where fishermen gather.
  • Local Festivals – If you visit during Mazu’s Birthday, you’ll see colorful boat processions.

Final Thoughts

Xiamen’s fishing culture is alive in its markets, villages, and even its modern districts. Whether you’re exploring historic ports or indulging in fresh seafood, each spot offers a unique window into this coastal way of life. So pack your camera, bring your appetite, and dive into Xiamen’s rich maritime heritage!
