Xiamen’s Top 5 Ancient Temples with Rich History

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Xiamen, a coastal city in Fujian Province, is not only famous for its stunning beaches and vibrant culture but also for its ancient temples that whisper tales of centuries past. These sacred sites are more than just places of worship—they are living museums of history, art, and spirituality. Whether you're a history buff, a spiritual seeker, or a curious traveler, exploring Xiamen’s top five ancient temples will transport you to a world where tradition and tranquility reign.

1. Nanputuo Temple (南普陀寺)

A Spiritual Oasis by the Sea

Perched at the foot of Wulao Peak and overlooking the shimmering waters of Xiamen Bay, Nanputuo Temple is one of the most revered Buddhist temples in southern China. Founded during the Tang Dynasty (618–907 AD), this temple has been a center of Buddhist learning and devotion for over a thousand years.

Highlights of Nanputuo Temple

  • Architectural Grandeur: The temple complex features traditional Chinese Buddhist architecture, with intricately carved eaves, golden statues, and serene courtyards. The main hall houses a magnificent statue of Guanyin, the Goddess of Mercy.
  • Vegetarian Cuisine: Don’t miss the temple’s famous vegetarian restaurant, where monks and visitors alike enjoy delicious, meat-free dishes that are as flavorful as they are wholesome.
  • Scenic Hiking Trails: Behind the temple, a stone staircase leads up Wulao Peak, offering panoramic views of Xiamen and the surrounding islands.

2. Kaiyuan Temple (开元寺)

A Glimpse into Quanzhou’s Golden Age

While technically located in nearby Quanzhou (a short trip from Xiamen), Kaiyuan Temple is too historically significant to leave off this list. Built in 686 AD during the Tang Dynasty, it was once the largest Buddhist temple in Fujian and a key stop along the Maritime Silk Road.

Why Kaiyuan Temple Stands Out

  • Ancient Pagodas: The temple grounds are home to twin stone pagodas, Zhenguo and Renshou, which date back to the Song Dynasty (960–1279 AD). These pagodas are adorned with intricate carvings depicting Buddhist lore and daily life in ancient China.
  • Cultural Fusion: Due to Quanzhou’s history as a global trade hub, the temple showcases a blend of Hindu, Islamic, and Buddhist influences in its art and architecture.
  • Sacred Bodhi Tree: A descendant of the original Bodhi tree under which Buddha attained enlightenment grows in the temple courtyard, adding to its spiritual aura.

3. Xiamen Tianzhu Temple (厦门天竺寺)

The Hidden Gem of Jimei

Tucked away in the Jimei District, Tianzhu Temple is a lesser-known but equally captivating site. Established during the Ming Dynasty (1368–1644), this temple is dedicated to the Taoist deity Mazu, the protector of seafarers.

What Makes Tianzhu Temple Special

  • Mazu Worship: As a coastal city, Xiamen has a deep connection to Mazu, and this temple is a prime spot to witness local devotion. During festivals, the temple comes alive with processions and offerings.
  • Serene Setting: Surrounded by lush greenery and a tranquil pond, the temple offers a peaceful retreat from the city’s hustle and bustle.
  • Unique Architecture: The temple’s design reflects traditional Fujianese style, with colorful ceramic figurines adorning its rooftops.

4. Hongshan Temple (鸿山寺)

Where History Meets Modernity

Located on Hongshan Mountain in central Xiamen, this temple dates back to the Tang Dynasty but has been meticulously restored to preserve its ancient charm while accommodating modern visitors.

Must-See Features of Hongshan Temple

  • Stunning City Views: The temple’s elevated position provides breathtaking vistas of Xiamen’s skyline and the distant sea.
  • Cultural Exhibits: The temple houses a small museum showcasing Buddhist artifacts and calligraphy from various dynasties.
  • Meditation Retreats: Visitors can participate in guided meditation sessions, offering a rare chance to unwind and reflect.

5. Qingjiao Cihui Temple (青礁慈济宫)

A Tribute to the Healing God

Situated in Haicang District, this temple honors Baosheng Dadi, a deity revered for his healing powers. Built during the Song Dynasty, it’s a masterpiece of Minnan (Southern Fujian) architecture.

Why Visit Qingjiao Cihui Temple

  • Healing Legends: The temple is steeped in folklore about miraculous cures, attracting those seeking blessings for health and well-being.
  • Vibrant Festivals: The annual Baosheng Cultural Festival features traditional performances, martial arts displays, and elaborate rituals.
  • Architectural Splendor: The temple’s ornate carvings, dragon pillars, and sweeping roofs are a photographer’s dream.

Tips for Visiting Xiamen’s Ancient Temples

  • Dress Modestly: As these are active places of worship, avoid shorts or sleeveless tops.
  • Respect Local Customs: Observe silence in prayer halls and follow any posted guidelines.
  • Timing Matters: Early mornings or weekdays are ideal for avoiding crowds.
  • Combine with Nearby Attractions: Pair temple visits with stops at Gulangyu Island, Hulishan Fortress, or Xiamen’s famed food streets.

Xiamen’s ancient temples are not just relics of the past—they are vibrant cultural hubs where history, faith, and beauty converge. Whether you’re lighting incense at Nanputuo or marveling at Kaiyuan’s pagodas, each temple offers a unique window into the soul of this enchanting city.
