Gulangyu Island’s Best Hiking Trails & Scenic Walks

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Gulangyu Island, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is a car-free paradise off the coast of Xiamen, China. Known for its colonial architecture, lush greenery, and coastal views, the island is a dream destination for hikers and walkers. Whether you're looking for challenging trails or leisurely strolls, Gulangyu offers something for everyone. Here’s your ultimate guide to the island’s best hiking trails and scenic walks.

Top Hiking Trails on Gulangyu

1. Sunlight Rock Trail

Difficulty: Moderate
Distance: 1.5 km (round trip)

Sunlight Rock (Riguang Yan) is the highest point on Gulangyu, offering panoramic views of the island and Xiamen’s skyline. The trail starts near the ferry pier and winds through dense foliage before reaching a series of stone steps leading to the summit.

Highlights:
- 360-degree views from the top, perfect for sunrise or sunset.
- Historical significance—the rock was once a strategic lookout point.
- Lush surroundings with tropical plants and occasional wildlife sightings.

Pro Tip: Arrive early to avoid crowds, especially during peak tourist seasons.

2. Shuzhuang Garden to Gangzihou Beach Trail

Difficulty: Easy
Distance: 2 km (one way)

This scenic walk connects two of Gulangyu’s most famous attractions: the elegant Shuzhuang Garden and the serene Gangzihou Beach. The trail meanders along the coastline, passing hidden coves and rocky outcrops.

Highlights:
- Shuzhuang Garden’s Piano Museum, a must-visit for music lovers.
- Seaside boardwalks with Instagram-worthy photo spots.
- Gangzihou Beach, a quiet escape from the busier parts of the island.

Pro Tip: Pack a picnic and enjoy it on the beach for a relaxing break.

3. The Hidden Forest Path (Lesser-Known Trail)

Difficulty: Moderate to Challenging
Distance: 3 km (loop)

For those seeking solitude, this lesser-known trail cuts through Gulangyu’s dense forests and abandoned colonial villas. It’s a bit overgrown in places, making it feel like a true adventure.

Highlights:
- Untouched nature with rare birds and butterflies.
- Abandoned mansions covered in vines—great for urban explorers.
- Quiet and peaceful, away from tourist hotspots.

Pro Tip: Wear sturdy shoes and bring insect repellent—this trail is wilder than the others.

Best Scenic Walks for Leisurely Exploration

1. Longtou Road Cultural Walk

Distance: 1.5 km (one way)

Longtou Road is Gulangyu’s main thoroughfare, lined with cafes, souvenir shops, and colonial-era buildings. While it’s busy during the day, an early morning or evening walk here is magical.

Highlights:
- Architectural gems like the Gulangyu Organ Museum.
- Local snacks—try Xiamen’s famous peanut soup or seafood noodles.
- Vibrant street art and live music performances in the evenings.

2. Coastal Promenade from Baozai Tower to Haoyue Park

Distance: 2.5 km (one way)

This breezy seaside walk offers stunning ocean views and passes by some of Gulangyu’s iconic landmarks.

Highlights:
- Baozai Tower, a quirky stone structure with folklore behind it.
- Haoyue Park, a romantic spot with a famous statue of Zheng Chenggong (Koxinga).
- Rocky shorelines perfect for tide-pooling.

3. The Piano Lane Walk

Distance: 1 km (loop)

Gulangyu is nicknamed "Piano Island" due to its musical heritage. This short walk takes you past historic homes where famous musicians once lived.

Highlights:
- Charming alleyways with piano-shaped tiles.
- Live piano music drifting from open windows.
- Quaint teahouses where you can relax with a cup of Tieguanyin.

Essential Tips for Hiking & Walking on Gulangyu

  • Best Time to Visit: Spring (March-May) and autumn (September-November) offer pleasant weather.
  • Footwear: Comfortable walking shoes are a must—some paths are uneven.
  • Hydration: Carry water, especially on hotter days.
  • Navigation: While the island is small, a map or offline GPS can help with lesser-known trails.
  • Ferry Tickets: Book in advance during holidays to avoid long waits.

Gulangyu’s trails and walks blend nature, history, and culture seamlessly. Whether you're climbing Sunlight Rock or strolling through Piano Lane, every step reveals something new. So lace up your shoes and explore this enchanting island on foot!
