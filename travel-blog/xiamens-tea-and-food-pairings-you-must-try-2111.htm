Xiamen’s Tea and Food Pairings You Must Try

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Xiamen, a coastal gem in Fujian Province, is not just about stunning beaches and colonial architecture. It’s a paradise for food lovers and tea enthusiasts. The city’s culinary scene is deeply intertwined with its tea culture, creating unforgettable flavor combinations. Whether you’re sipping on a fragrant Tieguanyin or indulging in local street food, Xiamen offers a symphony of tastes. Here’s your guide to the must-try tea and food pairings in this vibrant city.

The Art of Tea in Xiamen

Tea is more than a beverage in Xiamen—it’s a way of life. The city is famous for its oolong teas, particularly Tieguanyin and Dahongpao. These teas are not just enjoyed on their own but are often paired with local dishes to enhance flavors.

Tieguanyin: The Queen of Oolong

Tieguanyin, with its floral aroma and smooth finish, is a staple in Xiamen. It’s perfect for cutting through rich or greasy foods.

Best Pairings:
- Peanut Soup (花生汤, Huasheng Tang): The creamy sweetness of peanut soup contrasts beautifully with Tieguanyin’s slight astringency.
- Oyster Omelette (蚵仔煎, Ke Zai Jian): The tea’s floral notes balance the brininess of fresh oysters.

Dahongpao: The Bold and Earthy

Dahongpao, a roasted oolong, has a deep, smoky flavor. It’s ideal for hearty dishes.

Best Pairings:
- Braised Pork Belly (红烧肉, Hongshao Rou): The tea’s robustness complements the fatty richness of the pork.
- Fried Rice Noodles (炒米粉, Chao Mi Fen): Dahongpao’s smoky undertones elevate the umami of the noodles.

Street Food and Tea: A Match Made in Heaven

Xiamen’s street food is legendary, and pairing it with the right tea can take your experience to the next level.

Satay Noodles (沙茶面, Sha Cha Mian)

This spicy, nutty noodle dish is a Xiamen classic. Pair it with a light Jasmine Tea to soothe the heat and enhance the satay’s complexity.

Seafood Delights

Xiamen’s coastal location means seafood is everywhere. Try these pairings:
- Steamed Crab with Ginger: A delicate White Tea lets the crab’s sweetness shine.
- Grilled Squid: The smokiness calls for a Roasted Oolong like Shui Xian.

Dim Sum and Tea: A Cantonese Influence

While Xiamen has its own cuisine, Cantonese dim sum has made its mark. Here’s how to pair it:

Har Gow (Shrimp Dumplings)

The delicate shrimp filling pairs wonderfully with Tieguanyin, which cleanses the palate between bites.

Char Siu Bao (BBQ Pork Buns)

The sweet and savory filling is balanced by Dahongpao’s bold flavor.

Sweet Endings: Tea and Desserts

No meal is complete without dessert, and Xiamen’s sweets are no exception.

Mango Pomelo Sago (杨枝甘露, Yang Zhi Gan Lu)

This refreshing dessert is elevated with a cup of Jasmine Green Tea, which adds a floral note.

Peanut Rolls (花生卷, Huasheng Juan)

These crispy, sweet rolls are perfect with Milk Oolong, a creamy tea that mirrors the dessert’s richness.

Where to Experience These Pairings

For an authentic experience, visit these spots:
- Gulangyu Island: Tea houses here offer pairings with stunning sea views.
- Zhongshan Road: The night market is a great place to try street food with tea.
- Nanputuo Temple: Enjoy a serene tea session after exploring the temple.

Xiamen’s tea and food pairings are a journey for the senses. Whether you’re a tea connoisseur or a foodie, the city’s flavors will leave you craving more.
