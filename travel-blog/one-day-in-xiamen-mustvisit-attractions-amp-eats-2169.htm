One Day in Xiamen: Must-Visit Attractions & Eats

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Xiamen, a coastal gem in Fujian Province, is a city that effortlessly blends history, culture, and natural beauty. Whether you're a foodie, a history buff, or just someone looking to unwind by the sea, Xiamen has something for everyone. Here’s how to make the most of one unforgettable day in this vibrant city.

Morning: Exploring Gulangyu Island

Start with a Ferry Ride to Gulangyu

Kick off your day early with a short ferry ride to Gulangyu Island, a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The island is car-free, so be prepared to explore on foot. The morning light casts a magical glow over the colonial-era buildings, making it the perfect time for photography.

Must-See Spots on Gulangyu

  • Shuzhuang Garden: This seaside garden features intricate rock formations, lush greenery, and the iconic Piano Museum.
  • Sunlight Rock: Hike up to the highest point on the island for panoramic views of Xiamen’s skyline and the ocean.
  • Historic Villas: Wander through the narrow lanes lined with European-style villas, each with its own story.

Breakfast: Local Delicacies

Before heading back to the mainland, grab a quick bite at one of the island’s street vendors. Try shachamian (sand tea noodles), a unique Fujian dish, or peanut soup for a sweet start to your day.

Midday: Stroll Along Zhongshan Road & Nanputuo Temple

Zhongshan Road Pedestrian Street

Back in Xiamen proper, head to Zhongshan Road, the city’s most famous shopping street. The architecture here is a mix of old and new, with arcades reminiscent of Southeast Asia.

  • Shopping: Pick up souvenirs like oolong tea or Xiamen’s famous pineapple cakes.
  • Street Food: Don’t miss tusundong (sea worm jelly)—a local oddity—or the more approachable fried oyster omelet.

Nanputuo Temple

A short taxi ride away is Nanputuo Temple, one of China’s most revered Buddhist temples. The serene atmosphere and intricate carvings make it a peaceful retreat.

  • Climb the Mountain: Behind the temple, a trail leads to a viewpoint offering stunning city vistas.

Afternoon: Relax at Hulishan Fortress & Xiamen University

Hulishan Fortress

This coastal fortress, built in the late 19th century, features massive cannons and sweeping ocean views. It’s a great spot to learn about Xiamen’s maritime history.

Xiamen University

Often called the "most beautiful university in China," Xiamen University is worth a visit. Stroll through its palm-lined pathways and visit Furong Lake, where black swans glide across the water.

  • Pro Tip: The nearby Shapowei Art Zone is a hipster haven with murals, cafes, and boutique shops.

Evening: Sunset at Zengcuoan & Seafood Feast

Zengcuoan Village

This former fishing village is now a bohemian hotspot. Walk along the beach as the sun sets, then explore the quirky shops and art galleries.

Dinner: Seafood at Minnan Cuisine

End your day with a feast at a local seafood restaurant. Must-tries include:
- Steamed fish with ginger and scallions
- Stir-fried clams with black bean sauce
- Buddha Jumps Over the Wall, a luxurious Fujian soup

For dessert, try mugua (papaya) sweet soup or mango shaved ice from a street vendor.

Nightcap: Bar Hopping on Hubin Road

If you’re not ready to call it a night, head to Hubin Road for cocktails with a view. Rooftop bars like Luna Lounge offer skyline vistas, while speakeasies like Vanguard Bar serve creative drinks.

From sunrise to midnight, Xiamen delivers an unforgettable day of sights, flavors, and cultural gems. Pack your camera—and your appetite—and get ready to fall in love with this coastal paradise.
