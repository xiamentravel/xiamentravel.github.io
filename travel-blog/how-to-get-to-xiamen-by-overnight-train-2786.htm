How to Get to Xiamen by Overnight Train

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Xiamen, a coastal gem in Fujian Province, is a dream destination for travelers seeking a mix of urban charm and island serenity. While flights are quick, there’s something uniquely romantic about arriving by overnight train—watching the landscape shift from bustling cities to misty mountains as you drift to sleep. Here’s everything you need to know about taking an overnight train to Xiamen, plus tips to make your journey unforgettable.

Why Choose an Overnight Train to Xiamen?

Scenic & Relaxing

Unlike cramped flights, trains offer space to stretch your legs, socialize, and enjoy China’s diverse scenery. The route to Xiamen often passes through lush countryside, rolling hills, and even glimpses of the coastline as you near your destination.

Budget-Friendly

Overnight trains are a wallet-saver. For the price of a flight, you can book a comfortable sleeper berth and save on a night’s accommodation. Hard sleepers (硬卧, yìng wò) are the most economical, while soft sleepers (软卧, ruǎn wò) offer extra privacy.

Authentic Travel Experience

Trains are a staple of Chinese travel culture. Sharing a compartment with locals, snacking on instant noodles, and waking up to sunrise views is an adventure in itself.

Popular Overnight Train Routes to Xiamen

From Shanghai

The Z65 or K1209 trains depart Shanghai in the evening (around 6-8 PM) and arrive in Xiamen by mid-morning. The journey takes about 12-14 hours, perfect for a full night’s rest.

From Beijing

The K571 is a classic option, departing Beijing West Station in the late afternoon and arriving in Xiamen 24 hours later. Yes, it’s a long ride, but the gradual change in scenery—from northern plains to southern greenery—is worth it.

From Guangzhou/Shenzhen

The D688 or D2326 high-speed sleeper trains offer a quicker overnight option (8-10 hours) with modern amenities like charging ports and adjustable bunks.

Booking Your Overnight Train Ticket

Where to Buy

  • 12306.cn: The official railway site (English version available).
  • Trip.com or Ctrip: User-friendly for foreigners.
  • Station Counters: Ideal for last-minute tickets, but popular routes sell out fast.

Pro tip: Book 7-30 days in advance, especially during holidays like Golden Week or Spring Festival.

Choosing Your Berth

  • Hard Sleeper (6 beds/compartmen): Budget-friendly but noisy.
  • Soft Sleeper (4 beds/compartment): More privacy, slightly pricier.
  • Deluxe Soft Sleeper (2 beds): Rare but luxurious (found on some high-speed routes).

What to Pack for Your Overnight Train Adventure

Essentials

  • Earplugs & eye mask (trains can be noisy and bright).
  • Toiletries (wet wipes, toothbrush, hand sanitizer—some trains have basic sinks).
  • Snacks (cup noodles, fruit, or local treats like la tiao spicy strips).

Entertainment

  • Download movies or podcasts (Wi-Fi is spotty).
  • A book or travel journal.
  • A power bank (outlets are limited).

Onboard Etiquette & Survival Tips

Sharing Space Respectfully

  • Keep noise low after 10 PM.
  • Offer snacks to compartment mates—it’s a friendly gesture!
  • Shoes off inside the berth (slippers are handy).

Food on the Train

Dining cars serve hot meals (try the hongshao rou braised pork), but vendors also walk through with instant noodles, boiled eggs, and drinks. Pro move: Bring a reusable cup for hot water (free onboard).

Arriving in Xiamen: What’s Next?

Xiamen Railway Station vs. Xiamen North

Most overnight trains arrive at Xiamen Station (central location). Xiamen North is farther out but linked by metro.

First-Day Itinerary Ideas

  • Gulangyu Island: Take the ferry (10 mins from downtown) to this car-free UNESCO site.
  • Zhongshan Road: Stuff yourself with Fujianese street food (sha cha noodles, oyster omelets).
  • Nanputuo Temple: A serene start near Xiamen University.

Where to Stay

  • Budget: Hostels near Zengcuo’an Beach.
  • Mid-range: Boutique hotels in Siming District.
  • Splurge: The cozy Gulangyu Island guesthouses.

Final Pro Tips

  • Learn basic Mandarin phrases ("Xià yí zhàn shì nǎ lǐ?" = "Where’s the next stop?").
  • Arrive early to find your platform (stations can be chaotic).
  • Keep your ticket handy—you’ll need it to exit the station.

The overnight train to Xiamen isn’t just transport; it’s the first chapter of your adventure. From the rhythm of the tracks to the morning sea breeze, this journey is where the magic begins. All aboard!
