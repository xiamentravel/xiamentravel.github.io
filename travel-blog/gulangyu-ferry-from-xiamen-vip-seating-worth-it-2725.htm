Gulangyu Ferry from Xiamen: VIP Seating Worth It?

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Gulangyu, the car-free island just off the coast of Xiamen, is a must-visit for travelers exploring China’s Fujian province. Known for its colonial architecture, winding streets, and vibrant arts scene, the island attracts millions of visitors each year. But before you can explore its charm, you’ll need to take a ferry from Xiamen—and that’s where the question arises: Is VIP seating on the Gulangyu ferry worth the extra cost?

The Basics: Gulangyu Ferry Options

First, let’s break down the ferry options available. The standard ferry ride from Xiamen to Gulangyu is short (about 20 minutes) and affordable, with tickets starting as low as 35 RMB for a round trip. However, during peak seasons, the ferries can get crowded, with long lines and limited seating.

This is where VIP seating comes in. For roughly double the price (around 70-100 RMB), you can upgrade to a more comfortable experience. But what exactly does VIP seating offer, and is it worth the splurge?

What VIP Seating Includes

  1. Priority Boarding – Skip the long queues, especially during busy hours.
  2. Comfortable Seating – Plush chairs, often with better views.
  3. Air-Conditioned Lounges – Some ferries offer indoor VIP areas away from the crowds.
  4. Faster Service – Less waiting time for departure.

The Crowd Factor: When VIP Makes Sense

Peak Season vs. Off-Season

If you’re visiting Gulangyu during summer, Golden Week (October), or weekends, the ferry terminals can be chaotic. Long lines, packed boats, and limited seating make the standard ferry experience less enjoyable. In these cases, VIP seating can be a game-changer—saving you time and stress.

However, if you’re traveling on a weekday in the low season (winter or early spring), the standard ferry might be just fine. The crowds are thinner, and the extra cost may not be justified.

Traveling with Kids or Elderly Companions

Families with young children or elderly travelers might find VIP seating worthwhile. The priority boarding ensures you get seats together, and the more comfortable environment can make the short journey much smoother.

The Cost-Benefit Analysis

Is the Price Difference Justified?

At roughly 35 RMB vs. 70-100 RMB, the VIP upgrade isn’t exorbitant, but it’s still an additional expense. For budget travelers, the standard ticket is perfectly functional. However, if you value convenience and comfort, the extra 35-65 RMB could be a smart investment.

Alternative: Early Morning or Late Evening Ferries

Another way to avoid crowds without paying extra is to take the first ferry of the day (around 7:00 AM) or the last one (after 6:00 PM). These times are usually less crowded, and you might not need VIP seating at all.

The Experience: What Travelers Say

Online reviews from tourists are mixed. Some swear by VIP seating, calling it a "must" during peak times, while others say the standard ferry is perfectly fine. A few key takeaways:

  • "VIP was a lifesaver during Golden Week—no pushing, no stress."
  • "Not worth it if you’re traveling light and off-season."
  • "The AC lounge made the summer heat bearable."

Final Verdict: Who Should Book VIP?

  • Yes, if…

    • You’re visiting during peak season.
    • You dislike crowds and long waits.
    • You’re traveling with family or need extra comfort.

  • Skip it if…

    • You’re on a tight budget.
    • You’re traveling during off-peak hours.
    • You don’t mind standing for a short ride.

Ultimately, the decision comes down to personal preference and travel style. But for many, the small upgrade can make a big difference in starting the Gulangyu adventure on the right foot.
