BRT (Bus Rapid Transit) from Xiamen Airport – Is It Worth It?

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Xiamen, a coastal gem in Fujian Province, is a top-tier tourist destination known for its blend of urban charm and island serenity. Whether you're heading to Gulangyu Island, strolling along Zhongshan Road, or exploring the Tulou clusters, getting around efficiently is key. One of the most debated options for travelers is the BRT (Bus Rapid Transit) system, especially the route from Xiamen Gaoqi International Airport. But is it worth it? Let’s break it down.

What Is Xiamen’s BRT System?

Xiamen’s BRT isn’t your average bus service. It’s a high-capacity, dedicated-lane rapid transit system that mimics the efficiency of a metro but at a fraction of the cost. The BRT lanes are elevated or separated from regular traffic, meaning fewer delays and faster commutes. The system connects major hubs, including the airport, downtown Xiamen, and key suburban areas.

Key Features of Xiamen BRT

  • Dedicated Lanes: No getting stuck in traffic.
  • Frequent Service: Buses arrive every 3-5 minutes during peak hours.
  • Affordable Fares: Starts at just ¥0.5 (yes, really).
  • Smart Card Integration: Works with Xiamen’s public transport card (e.g., E-Tong Card).

The Airport BRT Route: BRT Line 1 (First Link)

The most relevant BRT line for travelers is BRT Line 1 (First Link), which runs from Xiamen North Railway Station to Xiamen Gaoqi Airport. The airport stop is conveniently located near Terminal 3 and Terminal 4, making it a viable option for arrivals and departures.

Pros of Taking the BRT from Xiamen Airport

  1. Cost-Effective

    • A taxi from the airport to downtown (e.g., Zhongshan Road) can cost ¥50-¥80. The BRT? Just ¥4-¥6.
    • If you’re on a budget, this is a no-brainer.

  2. Avoids Traffic Jams

    • Xiamen’s roads can get congested, especially during rush hour. The BRT’s dedicated lanes ensure a smooth ride.

  3. Scenic Views

    • The elevated sections offer glimpses of Xiamen’s skyline and coastline—great for first-time visitors.

  4. Easy Transfers

    • Connects seamlessly to metro lines (e.g., Metro Line 1) and other BRT routes.

Cons of Taking the BRT from Xiamen Airport

  1. Luggage Hassles

    • No dedicated luggage racks. If you’re carrying heavy suitcases, boarding and alighting can be awkward.

  2. Limited Late-Night Service

    • The last BRT from the airport departs around 11:30 PM. Late-night arrivals might need a taxi.

  3. Crowding During Peak Hours

    • Locals rely heavily on the BRT, so expect packed buses during rush hours (7-9 AM, 5-7 PM).

BRT vs. Taxi vs. Metro: Which Should You Choose?

Taxi (or Ride-Hailing)

  • Best for: Groups, late-night arrivals, or those with lots of luggage.
  • Drawback: Higher cost and potential traffic delays.

Metro (Line 1)

  • Best for: Travelers heading to downtown (e.g., Zhenhai Road Station for Zhongshan Road).
  • Drawback: Doesn’t directly serve the airport—requires a short taxi or bus transfer.

BRT

  • Best for: Budget travelers, solo explorers, or those who enjoy public transport experiences.
  • Drawback: Less convenient for heavy luggage.

Insider Tips for Using Xiamen’s BRT

  • Buy an E-Tong Card: Saves time and offers small fare discounts.
  • Avoid Rush Hour: If possible, travel outside peak times for a more comfortable ride.
  • Use Google Maps or AMAP: Real-time tracking helps plan your route.

Final Verdict: Is It Worth It?

For budget-conscious travelers or those who love efficient public transport, the BRT is a fantastic option. It’s fast, cheap, and offers a unique way to see the city. However, if you’re lugging around multiple suitcases or arriving late at night, a taxi might be the better choice.

Either way, Xiamen’s BRT is a testament to the city’s forward-thinking urban planning—and trying it at least once is part of the adventure.
