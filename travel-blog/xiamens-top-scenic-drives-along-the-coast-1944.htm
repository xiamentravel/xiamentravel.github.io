Xiamen’s Top Scenic Drives Along the Coast

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Xiamen, a coastal gem in Fujian Province, is a dream destination for road trippers. With its winding coastal roads, lush greenery, and breathtaking ocean views, driving here feels like navigating a postcard. Whether you're a local or a visitor, these scenic routes offer an unforgettable experience. Below, we’ve curated the best coastal drives in Xiamen, complete with must-see stops, hidden gems, and insider tips.

1. Island Ring Road: The Ultimate Coastal Cruise

Why It’s a Must-Drive

The Island Ring Road is Xiamen’s crown jewel for scenic drives. Stretching approximately 43 kilometers, this well-paved route loops around the entire island, offering uninterrupted views of the ocean, sandy beaches, and urban skyline. It’s perfect for a leisurely drive, a bike ride, or even a sunrise jog.

Top Stops Along the Way

  • Hulishan Fortress: A historic military site with panoramic sea views.
  • Zengcuoan Art Village: A bohemian enclave filled with murals, cafes, and indie shops.
  • Xiamen University Beach: A serene spot to stretch your legs and dip your toes in the sand.

Pro Tip

Avoid weekends if you prefer fewer crowds. Early mornings or weekdays offer smoother drives and better photo ops.

2. Huandao Road: Where the Mountains Meet the Sea

The Scenic Breakdown

Huandao Road (also part of the Island Ring Road) is a shorter but equally stunning segment, hugging the southeastern coastline. It’s particularly famous for its dramatic cliffs, secluded coves, and lush hillsides.

Don’t-Miss Attractions

  • Taiwan Folk Village: A cultural park showcasing Minnan and Taiwanese traditions.
  • Music Square: A quirky seaside plaza with giant piano-key walkways.
  • Huangcuo Beach: A quieter alternative to the busier beaches, ideal for a picnic.

Best Time to Go

Sunset drives here are magical—golden hour paints the ocean in hues of amber and pink.

3. The Gulangyu Loop: A Short but Sweet Escape

Why Drive Here?

While Gulangyu Island is mostly car-free, the short ferry ride from Xiamen’s mainland to the island’s perimeter road is worth it. The loop around the island takes about 20 minutes by electric cart (the only motorized option), offering glimpses of colonial architecture and the shimmering sea.

Highlights

  • Shuzhuang Garden: A cliffside garden with hidden caves and ocean vistas.
  • Sunlight Rock: The highest point on the island, with 360-degree views.
  • Foreign Consulates Historic District: A stroll through time with well-preserved 19th-century buildings.

Insider Advice

Book ferry tickets in advance—Gulangyu limits daily visitors.

4. The Xiang’an Tunnel Route: A Modern Marvel

What Makes It Special

Connecting Xiamen Island to Xiang’an District, this undersea tunnel is an engineering feat. The drive itself is quick (about 10 minutes), but the surrounding coastal roads leading to it are equally impressive.

Pit Stops to Explore

  • Xiang’an Wetland Park: A serene escape with boardwalks and migratory birds.
  • Datong Ancient Town: A lesser-known historic area with Ming-era charm.

Fun Fact

The tunnel is one of China’s first undersea roadways, making it a cool bucket-list drive.

5. The Jimei Coastal Drive: Culture and Coastline Combined

The Vibe

Jimei, a district just north of Xiamen Island, blends coastal scenery with rich cultural heritage. The drive here is dotted with temples, universities, and waterfront parks.

Key Attractions

  • Jimei School Village: A stunning campus designed by Tan Kah Kee, a famed philanthropist.
  • Ao Garden: A lakeside retreat with traditional pavilions and bridges.
  • Xinglin Bay Waterfront: A newly developed promenade with cafes and art installations.

When to Visit

Spring and autumn are ideal—pleasant weather and fewer tourists.

6. The Wanshi Botanical Garden Detour: A Green Escape

Why Detour Here?

While not strictly coastal, the roads leading to Wanshi Botanical Garden offer sweeping views of Xiamen’s skyline against the ocean. The lush, winding ascent feels like a tropical getaway.

Must-See Spots

  • Rock Wonderland: A surreal area with giant boulders and hidden trails.
  • South Putuo Temple: A peaceful Buddhist temple nestled in the hills.

Travel Hack

Visit on weekdays to avoid the crowds that flock here for weekend hikes.

Final Thoughts on Coastal Driving in Xiamen

Xiamen’s coastal drives are more than just routes—they’re experiences. From the iconic Island Ring Road to the hidden gems of Jimei, each journey offers a unique blend of nature, culture, and adventure. So fuel up, roll down the windows, and let Xiamen’s coastline take your breath away.
