Xiamen’s Public Transport for Families with Kids

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Xiamen, a coastal gem in Fujian Province, is a dream destination for families. With its pristine beaches, lush parks, and rich cultural heritage, it’s no wonder parents flock here with their little ones. But navigating a new city with kids can be daunting—especially when it comes to public transport. Fear not! Xiamen’s family-friendly transit system makes exploring the city a breeze. Here’s everything you need to know to get around smoothly.

Why Xiamen’s Public Transport is Perfect for Families

Xiamen’s public transport network is efficient, affordable, and designed with convenience in mind. From spotless metro lines to scenic bus routes, the city offers plenty of options for families. Here’s why it stands out:

  • Clean and Safe: Stations and vehicles are well-maintained, with priority seating for parents and kids.
  • Stroller-Friendly: Many buses and metro stations have ramps and elevators.
  • Affordable: Kids under 1.2 meters ride free on buses and the metro.
  • Scenic Routes: Some bus lines offer breathtaking views of the coastline.

Getting Around: The Best Options for Families

1. Xiamen Metro: Fast and Kid-Approved

The Xiamen Metro is a top choice for families. With two main lines (Line 1 and Line 2), it connects major attractions like Zhongshan Road, Hulishan Fortress, and Xiamen University.

Tips for Riding the Metro with Kids:

  • Avoid Rush Hour: Trains get crowded between 7:30–9:00 AM and 5:00–7:00 PM.
  • Use Elevators: Stations like Zhenhai Road and Hubin have elevators for strollers.
  • Buy a Tourist Pass: Unlimited rides for 24/72 hours (great for sightseeing).

2. Buses: The Scenic Way to Explore

Xiamen’s buses are a fantastic way to see the city. Route 29, for example, takes you along the stunning Huandao Road (Island Ring Road), passing beaches and parks.

Family-Friendly Bus Routes:

  • Route 18: Connects Gulangyu Ferry Terminal to Xiamen University.
  • Route 47: A scenic ride to Wanshi Botanical Garden.
  • B1 Rapid Bus: Express service to popular spots like Zengcuoan.

Pro Tips:

  • Front Seats: Grab the front seats on double-decker buses for the best views.
  • Off-Peak Travel: Buses are less crowded mid-morning or early afternoon.

3. Ferries to Gulangyu: A Fun Adventure

No trip to Xiamen is complete without visiting Gulangyu Island. The short ferry ride is a hit with kids—just be sure to book tickets in advance (they sell out fast!).

Ferry Tips:

  • Dongdu Pier: The main departure point; arrive 30 minutes early.
  • Priority Boarding: Families with strollers get early access.
  • Night Ferries: Less crowded and offer beautiful skyline views.

Kid-Friendly Attractions Accessible by Public Transport

1. Hulishan Fortress (Metro Line 1, Hubin Station)

This historic fort has cannons and tunnels that kids love. The nearby beach is perfect for a post-exploration picnic.

2. Xiamen Science and Technology Museum (Bus Route 24)

Interactive exhibits make learning fun. The museum is stroller-friendly with plenty of rest areas.

3. Riyuegu Hot Springs (Bus Route 805)

A relaxing escape with kid-safe pools and water play zones.

Practical Tips for Stress-Free Travel

  • Pack Light: Bring a compact stroller for buses and metro.
  • Snacks and Water: Keep kids energized during transit.
  • Use Apps: Xiamen Transit and Amap provide real-time updates.
  • Teach Basic Phrases: Simple Mandarin like "Xièxiè" (thank you) goes a long way.

With these tips, your family’s Xiamen adventure will be smooth, fun, and unforgettable. Happy travels!
