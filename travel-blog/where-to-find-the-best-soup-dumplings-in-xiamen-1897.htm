Where to Find the Best Soup Dumplings in Xiamen

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Xiamen, a coastal gem in Fujian province, is famous for its stunning beaches, colonial architecture, and vibrant food scene. While seafood often steals the spotlight, the city also boasts an incredible selection of soup dumplings (xiaolongbao). These delicate, broth-filled parcels are a must-try for any food lover visiting Xiamen. Whether you're a first-time visitor or a seasoned traveler, here’s your ultimate guide to finding the best soup dumplings in the city.

The Art of Xiaolongbao

Before diving into the best spots, let’s appreciate what makes xiaolongbao so special. Unlike regular dumplings, these are filled with a savory broth that bursts with flavor upon the first bite. The thin, pleated wrapper holds a mixture of minced meat (usually pork) and gelatinized broth, which turns liquid when steamed. Eating them requires a bit of finesse—pierce the skin gently, let the steam escape, and savor the rich broth before devouring the rest.

Key Features of Great Soup Dumplings

  • Thin, delicate wrappers that don’t tear easily
  • Generous, flavorful broth inside
  • Fresh, high-quality fillings with the right balance of meat and seasoning
  • Perfect steaming technique to ensure even cooking

Top Spots for Soup Dumplings in Xiamen

1. Din Tai Fung (鼎泰丰)

Located in SM Mall, this internationally renowned chain needs no introduction. While not a local Xiamen brand, Din Tai Fung’s xiaolongbao are consistently excellent. Their pork dumplings are a classic, with a perfect broth-to-filling ratio and ultra-thin skins. Pro tip: Pair them with their signature spicy cucumber salad for a refreshing contrast.

Why it’s worth visiting:
- Impeccable consistency
- Hygienic and comfortable dining environment
- Great for travelers who want a familiar yet high-quality experience

2. Jiahe Xiaolongbao (佳禾小笼包)

Tucked away in a bustling alley near Zhongshan Road, this humble spot is a local favorite. Unlike the upscale Din Tai Fung, Jiahe offers a more authentic, no-frills experience. Their crab roe xiaolongbao is a standout—rich, umami-packed, and incredibly satisfying.

What to try:
- Crab roe xiaolongbao (蟹粉小笼包)
- Truffle-infused pork dumplings (for a luxurious twist)
- Homemade chili oil to add a kick

3. Yonghe Dawang (永和大王)

Though primarily known for its Taiwanese-style breakfast, this chain also serves surprisingly good xiaolongbao. The broth is lighter than traditional versions, making it a great choice for those who prefer a less heavy meal.

Best for:
- A quick, affordable bite
- Pairing with soy milk or congee
- Late-night cravings (some branches are open 24/7)

4. Xiamen University Canteen Hidden Gem

For budget-conscious travelers, the canteens near Xiamen University offer cheap yet delicious xiaolongbao. The student crowd ensures freshness, and the prices are unbeatable. Don’t expect fancy decor, but do expect hearty, satisfying dumplings.

Why students love it:
- Under ¥10 for a steamer basket
- Quick service between classes
- Variety of dipping sauces to customize your meal

Beyond Xiaolongbao: Other Must-Try Dumplings in Xiamen

While xiaolongbao are the star, Xiamen’s dumpling scene doesn’t stop there. Here are a few other varieties worth sampling:

Har Gao (虾饺)

These translucent shrimp dumplings are a Cantonese classic but widely available in Xiamen. Look for them at dim sum spots or seafood restaurants.

Shengjianbao (生煎包)

Unlike xiaolongbao, these are pan-fried, resulting in a crispy bottom and juicy interior. Perfect for those who love texture contrasts.

Wontons (云吞)

Lighter than dumplings, these are often served in a clear broth with scallions and seaweed. A comforting option for a lighter meal.

Tips for Enjoying Soup Dumplings Like a Pro

  1. Use the spoon: Place the dumpling on a spoon to catch any spilled broth.
  2. Nibble carefully: Bite a small hole to release steam before eating.
  3. Dip wisely: A mix of black vinegar and ginger enhances the flavor without overpowering it.
  4. Eat fresh: Xiaolongbao are best enjoyed straight from the steamer—avoid letting them sit too long.

Final Bite

From high-end restaurants to street-side stalls, Xiamen offers a diverse range of soup dumplings to suit every palate and budget. Whether you’re exploring the historic Gulangyu Island or shopping along Zhongshan Road, make sure to indulge in this iconic dish. Each bite is a little taste of culinary craftsmanship—one that’s well worth seeking out.
