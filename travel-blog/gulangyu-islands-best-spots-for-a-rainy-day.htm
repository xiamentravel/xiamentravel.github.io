Gulangyu Island’s Best Spots for a Rainy Day

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Gulangyu Island, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is a dream destination for travelers seeking history, culture, and stunning coastal views. But what happens when the weather doesn’t cooperate? Rainy days on the island don’t have to dampen your spirits—instead, they offer a unique opportunity to explore Gulangyu’s hidden gems in a more intimate way. Here’s your guide to making the most of a drizzly day on this enchanting island.

Cozy Cafés and Tea Houses

1. Babycat Café

A beloved spot among locals and tourists alike, Babycat Café is the perfect retreat when the rain starts pouring. Known for its fluffy resident cats and artisanal pastries, this café exudes warmth and charm. Sip on a cup of locally sourced oolong tea or indulge in their famous pineapple cake while watching the raindrops dance on the cobblestone streets outside.

2. Naya Family Café

Tucked away in a colonial-era villa, Naya Family Café blends vintage aesthetics with modern comfort. Their hand-drip coffee is a must-try, and the cozy indoor seating makes it an ideal spot to journal, read, or simply enjoy the rhythmic sound of rain against the windows.

3. Gulangyu Tea Culture Center

For a deeper dive into Fujian’s tea traditions, head to the Gulangyu Tea Culture Center. Participate in a traditional tea ceremony and learn about the art of brewing Tieguanyin, a premium oolong variety. The serene ambiance and fragrant teas make this a soul-soothing escape from the rain.

Museums and Cultural Havens

1. Gulangyu Piano Museum

Gulangyu is often called the "Piano Island" due to its deep musical heritage. The Piano Museum houses an impressive collection of antique pianos, some dating back to the 18th century. The soft melodies playing in the background and the elegant interiors create a magical atmosphere, perfect for a rainy afternoon.

2. Shuzhuang Garden & Organ Museum

This former private villa turned museum is a masterpiece of landscape design. Wander through the lush gardens (with an umbrella in hand) and explore the Organ Museum, which features rare self-playing organs from around the world. The fusion of nature and music here is nothing short of enchanting.

3. Gulangyu Museum

To understand the island’s rich history, visit the Gulangyu Museum. Exhibits cover everything from its colonial past to its role as a hub for overseas Chinese. The museum’s quiet halls and well-curated displays make it a great place to spend a few reflective hours.

Indoor Shopping and Souvenir Hunting

1. Longtou Road’s Covered Market

Even in the rain, Longtou Road remains lively. The covered market section is packed with boutique shops selling handmade crafts, local snacks, and quirky souvenirs. Don’t miss the chance to pick up some Gulangyu-themed postcards or a jar of Fujian’s famous Minnan preserves.

2. Zhushan Road’s Art Studios

Gulangyu has long been a haven for artists, and Zhushan Road is where you’ll find their studios. Browse paintings, ceramics, and handmade jewelry while chatting with the creators. Many studios also offer short workshops—why not try your hand at calligraphy or pottery while waiting for the rain to pass?

3. Sunlight Rock Mall

For a more modern shopping experience, Sunlight Rock Mall offers a mix of local and international brands. Grab a bubble tea and explore the indoor stalls selling everything from stylish accessories to Gulangyu’s signature coconut candies.

Rainy-Day Eats: Comfort Food on Gulangyu

1. Zhang Sanfeng Oyster Omelette

A rainy day calls for hearty comfort food, and Zhang Sanfeng’s oyster omelette is a local legend. Crispy on the outside, tender on the inside, and packed with fresh seafood, this dish is best enjoyed hot with a side of spicy sauce.

2. Ye’s Braised Pork Rice

Another must-try is Ye’s Braised Pork Rice, a Fujianese classic. The melt-in-your-mouth pork belly, slow-cooked in a savory-sweet sauce, is the ultimate rainy-day indulgence. Pair it with a steaming bowl of herbal soup for extra warmth.

3. Lianhuan Seafood Noodles

For something lighter but equally satisfying, Lianhuan Seafood Noodles hits the spot. The broth is rich with umami flavors from shrimp, clams, and squid, while the handmade noodles add a comforting chewiness.

Relaxing Retreats

1. Gulangyu Bookworm

This quaint bookstore-café is a haven for bibliophiles. Curl up with a book from their well-stocked shelves, or join one of their occasional poetry readings. The soft hum of conversation and the scent of old books create a wonderfully cozy vibe.

2. Island Spa & Wellness Center

Turn a rainy day into a pampering session at one of Gulangyu’s spa retreats. Try a traditional Chinese massage or a soothing herbal foot soak to unwind. Many spas also offer views of the misty coastline, adding to the tranquil experience.

3. Rainy-Day Photography

Don’t let the rain stop you from capturing Gulangyu’s beauty. The glistening streets, fog-draped hills, and moody coastal views make for stunning photographs. Pack a waterproof camera or phone case and embrace the ethereal atmosphere.

Rain or shine, Gulangyu Island never fails to captivate. With these indoor-friendly activities, a drizzly day can become one of the most memorable parts of your trip. So grab an umbrella, step into the mist, and discover the island’s quieter, more introspective side.
