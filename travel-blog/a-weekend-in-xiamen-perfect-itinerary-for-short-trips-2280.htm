A Weekend in Xiamen: Perfect Itinerary for Short Trips

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Xiamen, a coastal gem in Fujian Province, is the ideal destination for a short getaway. With its blend of colonial charm, vibrant food scene, and stunning island views, this city offers something for every traveler. Whether you're a history buff, a foodie, or just looking to relax by the sea, here’s how to make the most of a weekend in Xiamen.

Day 1: Exploring Gulangyu & Zhongshan Road

Morning: Ferry to Gulangyu Island

Start your day early by catching a ferry to Gulangyu Island, a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The island is car-free, so be prepared to explore on foot. Wander through its winding alleys lined with colonial-era villas, many of which have been converted into museums, cafes, and boutique hotels.

Don’t miss:
- Shuzhuang Garden: A beautifully landscaped garden with ocean views.
- Piano Museum: A tribute to Gulangyu’s musical heritage.
- Sunlight Rock: Hike up for panoramic views of the island and Xiamen’s skyline.

Afternoon: Seafood Lunch & Beach Time

After working up an appetite, head to Lianhua Seafood Market or a local restaurant for fresh seafood. Try Xiamen specialties like oyster omelet (蚵仔煎, pronounced ô-á-jiān) or steamed crab with glutinous rice.

Post-lunch, relax at Gulangyu’s beaches. While not the best for swimming, Gangzaihou Beach offers a peaceful retreat with soft sand and gentle waves.

Evening: Zhongshan Road Night Market

Return to the mainland and dive into Zhongshan Road Pedestrian Street, Xiamen’s most famous shopping and food street. As night falls, the area transforms into a lively night market.

Must-try street foods:
- Peanut soup (花生汤, huāshēng tāng) – A sweet, creamy local favorite.
- Sha Cha Noodles (沙茶面, shā chá miàn) – A savory noodle dish with a rich peanut-based broth.
- Fried taro balls (炸芋枣, zhá yù zǎo) – Crispy on the outside, soft inside.

Day 2: Nanputuo Temple & Xiamen University

Morning: Spiritual Start at Nanputuo Temple

Begin your second day at Nanputuo Temple, one of China’s most famous Buddhist temples. Nestled at the foot of Wulao Peak, the temple complex features intricate carvings, serene courtyards, and a vegetarian restaurant.

Pro tip: Climb the Wulao Peak behind the temple for a stunning sunrise view over Xiamen.

Late Morning: Stroll Through Xiamen University

Just a short walk from Nanputuo Temple is Xiamen University (XMU), often called the "most beautiful campus in China." Its lush gardens, colonial architecture, and lakeside pathways make it a perfect spot for a leisurely walk.

Highlights:
- Furong Tunnel: A student-decorated tunnel filled with colorful graffiti.
- Lover’s Lake: A picturesque spot popular with couples.

Afternoon: Coffee Break & Huandao Road

After exploring XMU, recharge at one of Xiamen’s trendy cafes. The city has a thriving coffee culture, with spots like 32/HOW and Barista Honor serving top-notch brews.

Then, rent a bike and ride along Huandao Road (Island Ring Road), a scenic coastal route. Stop at Hulishan Fortress for a glimpse of Xiamen’s military history and panoramic ocean views.

Evening: Sunset at Zengcuoan & Craft Beer

Wrap up your weekend with a visit to Zengcuoan, Xiamen’s artsy fishing village turned hipster hub. Stroll through its narrow lanes filled with indie shops, murals, and seafood stalls.

For dinner, try Xiamen-style hot pot or grab a craft beer at Mellow Mood, a local favorite. End the night by the beach, watching the sunset over the Taiwan Strait.

Bonus Tips for a Smooth Trip

  • Transport: Use Didi (China’s Uber) or the efficient bus system.
  • Best Time to Visit: Spring (March-May) or Autumn (October-November) for pleasant weather.
  • Language: While English isn’t widely spoken, translation apps like Google Translate or Pleco can help.

With this itinerary, you’ll experience the best of Xiamen in just two days—leaving you refreshed, well-fed, and already planning your next visit.
