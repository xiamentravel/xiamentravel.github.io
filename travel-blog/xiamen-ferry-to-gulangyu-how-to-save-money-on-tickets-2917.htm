Xiamen Ferry to Gulangyu: How to Save Money on Tickets

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Gulangyu, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is one of China’s most charming island destinations. Just a short ferry ride from Xiamen, this car-free island is famous for its colonial architecture, piano-filled streets, and stunning coastal views. But before you set foot on this paradise, you’ll need to secure a ferry ticket—and prices can add up if you’re not careful. Here’s how to save money on Xiamen ferry tickets to Gulangyu without sacrificing convenience.

Understanding the Ferry Routes

Before booking, it’s essential to know the different ferry routes and terminals. Not all ferries are created equal, and choosing the right one can save you both time and money.

Main Ferry Terminals in Xiamen

  1. Xiamen International Cruise Center (Dongdu Pier) – The most popular departure point for tourists, offering direct routes to Gulangyu’s Sanqiutian Pier or Neicuo’ao Pier.
  2. Lun Du Ferry Terminal – Primarily serves locals, with cheaper tickets but fewer departures.
  3. Songyu Pier – A less crowded alternative, ideal for those staying in eastern Xiamen.

Gulangyu Arrival Piers

  • Sanqiutian Pier – Closest to major attractions like Shuzhuang Garden and Piano Museum.
  • Neicuo’ao Pier – Better for those heading to the quieter, southern part of the island.

Ticket Types and Pricing

Ferry tickets to Gulangyu come in different tiers, and knowing the differences can help you pick the most budget-friendly option.

Standard Economy Tickets (¥35-50 Round Trip)

These are the most affordable but sell out quickly, especially during peak seasons. They offer basic seating with no frills.

Luxury Cabin Tickets (¥50-100+)

For a more comfortable ride, some ferries offer upgraded cabins with air conditioning and better views. However, unless you’re traveling in extreme heat, this isn’t necessary for the short 20-minute ride.

Night Cruise Tickets (¥60-120)

If you’re visiting Gulangyu in the evening, some ferries offer night cruises with city light views. While scenic, these are pricier and not the best choice for budget travelers.

How to Save Money on Ferry Tickets

1. Book Online in Advance

The official WeChat mini-program (厦门轮渡有限公司) or platforms like Ctrip (Trip.com) often have discounts for early bookings. Last-minute tickets at the pier can be more expensive or even sold out.

2. Avoid Peak Travel Times

Weekends, Chinese holidays (Golden Week, Spring Festival), and summer vacations see inflated prices. If possible, visit on a weekday for cheaper fares.

3. Use the Local Terminal (Lun Du Pier)

Tourists usually default to the International Cruise Center, but Lun Du Pier offers the same route for nearly half the price (¥8-15 one way). The catch? Fewer departures and longer queues.

4. Skip the Guided Tours

Some agencies bundle ferry tickets with island tours at a markup. Unless you need a guide, buy tickets separately to save.

5. Consider a Combo Ticket

If you plan to visit multiple Xiamen attractions (like Hulishan Fortress or Nanputuo Temple), some travel passes include discounted ferry tickets.

Insider Tips for a Smooth Ferry Experience

Arrive Early (Especially for Same-Day Tickets)

Even with an online booking, arrive at least 30 minutes early—boarding can be chaotic, and ferries leave on strict schedules.

Bring Your Passport or ID

Foreign travelers must present their passport for ticket verification. Without it, you won’t be allowed to board.

Pack Light (Or Be Ready to Pay Extra)

Large luggage may incur additional fees. Since Gulangyu is pedestrian-only, a backpack is ideal.

Check the Weather Before Booking

Ferries may cancel during storms or heavy fog, leaving you stranded. Always have a backup plan.

Alternative Ways to Explore Gulangyu

If ferries feel too restrictive, consider these options:

Private Boats (¥200-500 per group)

Small groups can charter private boats from fishermen, but this is significantly more expensive.

Walking Tours from Xiamen’s Coastline

While you can’t walk to Gulangyu, the Xiamen-Gulangyu pedestrian bridge (if ever completed) might change this in the future.

Final Thoughts on Budget Ferry Travel

Gulangyu is worth every penny, but smart travelers know how to cut costs without missing out. By booking early, choosing the right terminal, and avoiding tourist traps, you can enjoy this island gem without overspending.

Now that you’re armed with these tips, all that’s left is to board the ferry and let Gulangyu’s magic take over. Happy travels!
