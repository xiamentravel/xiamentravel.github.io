Xiamen’s Most Scenic Hiking Trails

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Xiamen, a coastal city in Fujian Province, is renowned for its stunning landscapes, lush greenery, and vibrant culture. While the city is famous for attractions like Gulangyu Island and Nanputuo Temple, its hiking trails offer an equally captivating experience for outdoor enthusiasts. Whether you’re a seasoned hiker or a casual walker, Xiamen’s trails provide breathtaking views, fresh air, and a chance to connect with nature. Here’s a guide to the most scenic hiking trails in Xiamen.

1. Wanshi Botanical Garden Trail

Why It’s a Must-Visit

Nestled at the foot of Wanshi Mountain, the Wanshi Botanical Garden is a haven for plant lovers and hikers alike. The trail winds through dense forests, past tranquil ponds, and alongside exotic flora. The garden is home to over 6,000 plant species, making it one of the most biodiverse spots in Xiamen.

Trail Highlights

  • Rock Formations: The trail features unique granite boulders, some of which resemble animals or mythical creatures.
  • Lotus Ponds: Seasonal blooms create a picturesque setting, perfect for photography.
  • Panoramic Views: The higher sections of the trail offer sweeping views of Xiamen’s skyline.

Tips for Hikers

  • Wear comfortable shoes, as some sections are rocky.
  • Visit early in the morning to avoid crowds and enjoy the cool breeze.

2. Xiamen Island Ring Road Coastal Trail

A Scenic Coastal Adventure

This trail stretches along the coastline of Xiamen Island, offering uninterrupted views of the ocean. It’s a relatively flat path, making it ideal for beginners or those looking for a leisurely stroll. The trail is especially popular at sunrise and sunset, when the sky transforms into a canvas of vibrant colors.

Trail Highlights

  • Beach Access: Several spots along the trail lead to secluded beaches where you can take a break and dip your toes in the water.
  • Art Installations: The route is dotted with sculptures and art pieces that reflect Xiamen’s cultural heritage.
  • Cycling Friendly: If hiking isn’t your thing, rent a bike and enjoy the trail on two wheels.

Tips for Hikers

  • Bring sunscreen and a hat, as shade is limited.
  • Pack a picnic—there are plenty of scenic spots to stop and eat.

3. Yun Ding Mountain Trail

The Highest Peak in Xiamen

For those seeking a challenge, the Yun Ding Mountain Trail is the ultimate hike. At 339 meters above sea level, it’s the highest point on Xiamen Island. The trail is steep in places, but the reward is a 360-degree view of the city and surrounding islands.

Trail Highlights

  • Historic Sites: Along the way, you’ll pass remnants of old fortifications and temples.
  • Wildlife Spotting: Keep an eye out for rare birds and butterflies.
  • Sunrise Views: Many hikers tackle this trail before dawn to catch the sunrise from the summit.

Tips for Hikers

  • Bring plenty of water—there are no vendors along the trail.
  • Wear sturdy hiking boots for the rocky terrain.

4. Hulishan Fortress Trail

A Blend of History and Nature

This trail combines natural beauty with historical significance. Hulishan Fortress, built in the late 19th century, is a well-preserved coastal defense structure. The trail around the fortress offers stunning ocean views and a glimpse into Xiamen’s military past.

Trail Highlights

  • Cannon Displays: The fortress houses one of the largest ancient cannons in the world.
  • Cliffside Paths: Walk along the cliffs for dramatic views of the waves crashing below.
  • Lighthouse: The trail leads to a picturesque lighthouse, a great spot for photos.

Tips for Hikers

  • Visit on weekdays to avoid tourist crowds.
  • Combine your hike with a visit to the nearby Xiamen University campus.

5. Tianzhu Mountain Trail

A Hidden Gem

Tucked away in the northern part of Xiamen, Tianzhu Mountain is a lesser-known hiking spot that offers tranquility and unspoiled nature. The trail is shaded by dense forests, making it a cool retreat during the summer months.

Trail Highlights

  • Waterfalls: Seasonal rains create small waterfalls along the trail.
  • Bamboo Groves: The path winds through serene bamboo forests, perfect for meditation.
  • Local Tea Houses: Stop by a traditional tea house near the trailhead to sample Fujian’s famous oolong tea.

Tips for Hikers

  • Bring insect repellent—the forested areas can be buggy.
  • The trail is best visited in spring or autumn when temperatures are mild.

6. Jimei’s Dragon Pool Trail

A Cultural Hike

Located in the Jimei District, this trail is named after the legendary Dragon Pool, a natural spring surrounded by lush vegetation. The area is steeped in folklore, and the trail passes several temples and shrines.

Trail Highlights

  • Dragon Pool Temple: A small but beautifully decorated temple dedicated to local deities.
  • Stone Carvings: Ancient carvings along the trail tell stories of Xiamen’s past.
  • Local Snacks: Vendors near the trail sell traditional Fujian snacks like peanut soup and oyster omelets.

Tips for Hikers

  • Wear modest clothing if planning to visit temples.
  • The trail is relatively short, so it’s great for families with kids.

Final Thoughts

Xiamen’s hiking trails offer something for everyone—from coastal walks to mountain climbs, from historical explorations to peaceful forest retreats. Each trail provides a unique way to experience the city’s natural beauty and rich culture. So lace up your hiking boots, grab your camera, and hit the trails to discover Xiamen’s hidden treasures.
