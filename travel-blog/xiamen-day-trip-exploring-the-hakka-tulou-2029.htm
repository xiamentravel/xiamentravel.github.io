Xiamen Day Trip: Exploring the Hakka Tulou

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

If you're visiting Xiamen and looking for a unique cultural adventure, a day trip to the Hakka Tulou is an absolute must. These ancient earthen buildings, scattered across the lush mountains of Fujian Province, offer a fascinating glimpse into China’s rich heritage. With their circular or rectangular designs, the Tulou are not just architectural marvels but also living museums of Hakka culture.

Why Visit the Hakka Tulou?

The Hakka Tulou are UNESCO World Heritage-listed structures, built between the 12th and 20th centuries. These fortified communal homes were designed to house entire clans, providing protection, warmth, and a strong sense of community. Today, they stand as a testament to the ingenuity of the Hakka people.

Unique Architecture

One of the most striking features of the Tulou is their massive earthen walls, some as thick as six feet. The circular design, particularly seen in the famous Chengqi Lou and Zhencheng Lou, creates a harmonious living space where families shared kitchens, ancestral halls, and storage areas. The buildings are earthquake-resistant and climate-controlled—proof of ancient sustainable engineering.

Cultural Immersion

Visiting a Tulou isn’t just about admiring the architecture; it’s about stepping into a living community. Many Tulou are still inhabited by Hakka families who maintain traditional lifestyles. You might see elderly women drying tea leaves in the courtyard or children playing under the watchful eyes of their grandparents.

Planning Your Day Trip from Xiamen

A day trip to the Hakka Tulou is entirely feasible, but it requires some planning. The most popular clusters are in Yongding and Nanjing counties, about 2.5 to 3 hours from Xiamen by car.

Best Tulou to Visit

  1. Chengqi Lou (Yongding) – Known as the "King of Tulou," this four-story circular structure has over 400 rooms.
  2. Zhencheng Lou (Hongkeng Village) – A stunning blend of Chinese and Western architectural influences.
  3. Tianluokeng Tulou Cluster (Nanjing) – A picturesque group of five Tulou, often called the "Four Dishes and One Soup" due to their layout.

Transportation Options

  • Private Car or Taxi: The most convenient but expensive option (~¥800-1000 round trip).
  • Public Bus: Take a bus from Xiamen to Longyan or Yongding, then transfer to local transport (~¥100-150).
  • Guided Tours: Many agencies offer day trips (~¥300-500 per person), including transportation and a local guide.

Experiencing Hakka Culture

Once you arrive, immerse yourself in the local culture. Here’s how to make the most of your visit:

Try Hakka Cuisine

Hakka food is hearty and flavorful. Don’t miss:
- Lei Cha (Pounded Tea): A savory tea soup with nuts, seeds, and herbs.
- Mei Cai Kou Rou (Braised Pork with Preserved Vegetables): A rich, melt-in-your-mouth dish.
- Tofu Pudding: Silky and fresh, often served with ginger syrup.

Participate in Local Activities

Some Tulou offer hands-on experiences:
- Tea Tasting: Fujian is famous for its oolong tea, and many families will gladly brew a pot for visitors.
- Traditional Craft Workshops: Learn about Hakka embroidery or bamboo weaving.

Photography Tips

The Tulou are incredibly photogenic. For the best shots:
- Golden Hour: Early morning or late afternoon light enhances the earthy tones.
- Drone Shots: If permitted, aerial views showcase the geometric beauty of the clusters.
- Local Portraits: Capture candid moments of residents going about their daily lives.

Sustainable Tourism

As tourism grows, it’s important to visit responsibly:
- Respect Privacy: Ask before taking photos of residents.
- Support Local Businesses: Buy handmade souvenirs or dine at family-run restaurants.
- Leave No Trace: Avoid littering and follow designated paths.

Final Thoughts

A day trip to the Hakka Tulou from Xiamen is more than just sightseeing—it’s a journey through history, culture, and human resilience. Whether you’re an architecture enthusiast, a food lover, or simply a curious traveler, the Tulou will leave a lasting impression.
