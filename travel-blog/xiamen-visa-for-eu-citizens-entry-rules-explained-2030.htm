Xiamen Visa for EU Citizens: Entry Rules Explained

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Xiamen, a coastal gem in China’s Fujian Province, is a dream destination for travelers seeking a mix of urban sophistication and natural beauty. From the serene Gulangyu Island to the bustling Zhongshan Road, this city offers a unique blend of history, culture, and modernity. For EU citizens planning a trip, understanding the visa requirements is crucial. Here’s everything you need to know about securing a Xiamen visa and making the most of your visit.

Visa Requirements for EU Citizens

Do EU Citizens Need a Visa for Xiamen?

Yes, EU passport holders must obtain a visa before traveling to Xiamen, as China does not currently offer visa-free entry for most European nationals. However, there are exceptions for short layovers (up to 144 hours) under the Transit Without Visa (TWOV) policy if certain conditions are met.

Types of Chinese Visas for Xiamen

  1. Tourist Visa (L Visa) – The most common option for leisure travelers. It allows stays of up to 30, 60, or 90 days, depending on the issuance.
  2. Transit Visa (G Visa) – For travelers passing through China en route to another country.
  3. Business Visa (M Visa) – Required if visiting for work-related purposes.
  4. 144-Hour TWOV – Available for travelers transiting through major Chinese cities, including Xiamen, under specific conditions.

How to Apply for a Xiamen Visa

  1. Complete the Application Form – Fill out the Chinese visa application form online or at the consulate.
  2. Gather Required Documents –
    • Passport with at least six months validity and blank pages.
    • Recent passport-sized photo.
    • Proof of travel (flight itinerary).
    • Hotel reservations or invitation letter from a host in China.
  3. Submit to the Chinese Embassy/Consulate – Applications must be submitted in person or via an authorized visa agency.
  4. Pay the Visa Fee – Costs vary depending on visa type and processing speed.
  5. Wait for Processing – Typically takes 4-5 business days, with expedited options available.

Exploring Xiamen: Must-Visit Attractions

Gulangyu Island – A UNESCO World Heritage Site

Known as the "Piano Island," Gulangyu is a car-free haven with colonial architecture, winding alleys, and stunning ocean views. Don’t miss:

  • Shuzhuang Garden – A beautiful seaside garden with a piano museum.
  • Sunlight Rock – The highest point on the island, offering panoramic views.

Zhongshan Road – The Heart of Xiamen’s Food Scene

This pedestrian-friendly street is packed with local snacks, seafood restaurants, and traditional Fujianese cuisine. Must-try dishes include:

  • Shacha noodles – A savory noodle dish with a unique peanut-based sauce.
  • Oyster omelet – A crispy, flavorful street food staple.

Nanputuo Temple – A Spiritual Retreat

One of China’s most famous Buddhist temples, Nanputuo offers tranquility amid lush greenery. Visitors can explore its grand halls, intricate carvings, and even enjoy a vegetarian meal at the temple’s restaurant.

Xiamen University – A Campus Like No Other

Often ranked among China’s most beautiful universities, Xiamen University features stunning architecture, a serene lake, and a vibrant student atmosphere. The nearby Furong Tunnel, covered in student graffiti, is a hidden gem.

Travel Tips for EU Visitors

Best Time to Visit Xiamen

  • Spring (March-May) – Pleasant weather, blooming flowers.
  • Autumn (September-November) – Mild temperatures, fewer crowds.
    Avoid the summer months if you dislike humidity and peak tourist season.

Getting Around Xiamen

  • Public Buses & Metro – Affordable and efficient.
  • Taxis & Ride-Hailing – Convenient but ensure the driver uses the meter.
  • Biking – A great way to explore coastal routes.

Cultural Etiquette

  • Respect Local Customs – Dress modestly when visiting temples.
  • Bargaining – Common in markets but not in malls or restaurants.
  • Tipping – Not expected but appreciated in high-end establishments.

With its rich culture, stunning landscapes, and welcoming atmosphere, Xiamen is a destination worth the extra visa steps. Plan ahead, pack your curiosity, and get ready for an unforgettable journey.
