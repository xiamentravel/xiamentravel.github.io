Xiamen’s Natural Beauty: Scenic Attractions You’ll Love

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Xiamen, a coastal city in Fujian Province, is a gem of natural beauty and cultural charm. Known for its lush greenery, pristine beaches, and unique island landscapes, this city offers a perfect blend of relaxation and adventure. Whether you’re a nature lover, a history enthusiast, or someone seeking a peaceful getaway, Xiamen has something for everyone. Here are the must-visit scenic attractions that will leave you in awe.

Gulangyu Island: A Car-Free Paradise

Why Gulangyu Is a Must-Visit

Gulangyu, a small pedestrian-only island, is Xiamen’s crown jewel. With its colonial-era architecture, winding cobblestone streets, and vibrant arts scene, it feels like stepping into a different era. The island is also a UNESCO World Heritage Site, celebrated for its well-preserved historical buildings and lush gardens.

Top Spots on Gulangyu

  • Shuzhuang Garden: This seaside garden blends Chinese landscaping with ocean views, featuring hidden pathways, rock formations, and the famous Piano Museum.
  • Sunlight Rock: The highest point on the island offers panoramic views of Xiamen’s skyline and the surrounding sea.
  • Beaches: Gulangyu’s beaches, like Gangzaihou and Haoyueyuan, are perfect for a quiet stroll or a sunset picnic.

Xiamen Botanical Garden: A Green Oasis

Exploring the Garden’s Highlights

Spanning over 200 hectares, the Xiamen Botanical Garden is a paradise for plant lovers. The garden is divided into themed sections, each showcasing unique flora from around the world.

Don’t-Miss Sections

  • Cactus and Succulent Zone: Home to towering cacti and rare desert plants, this area feels like a slice of the American Southwest.
  • Rainforest Greenhouse: Walk through a misty, tropical environment filled with exotic plants and cascading waterfalls.
  • Bamboo Grove: A serene spot with over 200 bamboo species, perfect for meditation or photography.

Hulishan Fortress: Where History Meets the Sea

A Glimpse into Xiamen’s Military Past

Built in the late 19th century, Hulishan Fortress is one of China’s best-preserved coastal defense structures. The fortress features massive cannons, underground tunnels, and stunning views of the Taiwan Strait.

What to See Here

  • The Krupp Cannon: A massive German-made cannon, once the largest in Asia, is the fortress’s centerpiece.
  • Coastal Walkways: Stroll along the fortress walls for breathtaking ocean vistas.
  • Military Exhibits: Learn about Xiamen’s role in China’s coastal defense through interactive displays.

Wanshi Botanical Garden: A Hidden Gem

Why It’s Worth the Visit

Less crowded than the main botanical garden, Wanshi Botanical Garden offers a tranquil escape with its lotus ponds, bonsai collections, and shaded walking paths.

Highlights

  • Lotus Pond: Especially beautiful in summer when the flowers are in full bloom.
  • Bonsai Garden: Marvel at meticulously crafted miniature trees, some over a century old.
  • Hiking Trails: The garden connects to nearby hills, offering light hikes with scenic overlooks.

Zengcuoan: Xiamen’s Artistic Seaside Village

The Bohemian Vibe of Zengcuoan

Once a quiet fishing village, Zengcuoan has transformed into a hub for artists, musicians, and backpackers. Its narrow lanes are lined with quirky cafes, boutique shops, and vibrant street art.

What to Do Here

  • Beachfront Cafes: Sip coffee while watching the waves at one of the many seaside cafes.
  • Night Market: Sample local street food like oyster omelets and peanut soup.
  • Art Galleries: Discover works by local artists in small, independent galleries.

Xiamen’s Coastal Walkway: A Scenic Stroll

The Beauty of the Huandao Road

Stretching over 30 kilometers, Xiamen’s coastal walkway is one of the most picturesque urban trails in China. Whether you bike, jog, or walk, the route offers stunning views of the ocean, parks, and city skyline.

Best Sections to Explore

  • Music Square: A lively stretch with performances and public art installations.
  • Baicheng Beach: A popular spot for swimming and sunbathing.
  • Island Ring Road: Rent a bike and cycle along the waterfront for an unforgettable experience.

Nanputuo Temple: Serenity by the Mountains

A Spiritual Retreat

Nestled at the foot of Wulao Peak, Nanputuo Temple is one of Fujian’s most famous Buddhist temples. The serene atmosphere, intricate carvings, and surrounding greenery make it a perfect place for reflection.

Temple Highlights

  • Main Hall: Admire the golden statues of Buddha and the detailed murals.
  • Vegetarian Restaurant: Try the temple’s renowned vegetarian dishes, a must for foodies.
  • Hiking Trails: Climb the nearby peaks for sweeping views of Xiamen.

Xiamen’s Nightlife: Where Nature Meets Urban Energy

Best Spots for Evening Views

  • Yundang Lake: The lake’s boardwalk lights up at night, creating a romantic ambiance.
  • Zhongshan Road: This pedestrian street comes alive with food stalls, live music, and shopping.
  • Gulangyu at Night: The island takes on a magical glow after dark, with fewer crowds and cooler breezes.

Xiamen’s natural beauty is unmatched, offering everything from tranquil gardens to vibrant coastal scenes. Whether you’re exploring historic sites, relaxing on sandy beaches, or immersing yourself in local culture, this city promises an unforgettable travel experience.
