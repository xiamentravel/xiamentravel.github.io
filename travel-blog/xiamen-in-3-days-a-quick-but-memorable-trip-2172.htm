Xiamen in 3 Days: A Quick but Memorable Trip

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Xiamen, a coastal city in Fujian Province, is a perfect blend of urban charm and island serenity. Whether you're a history buff, a foodie, or a beach lover, this city has something for everyone. Here’s how to make the most of a three-day trip to Xiamen, covering must-visit spots, hidden gems, and local flavors.

Day 1: Exploring Gulangyu and Zhongshan Road

Morning: Gulangyu Island – The Piano Isle

Start your day early with a ferry ride to Gulangyu Island, a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Known as the "Piano Island," this car-free paradise is filled with colonial-era architecture, winding alleys, and lush gardens.

  • Shuzhuang Garden: A seaside garden with hidden caves and stunning ocean views.
  • Sunlight Rock: Climb to the top for a panoramic view of Xiamen’s skyline.
  • Piano Museum: A treat for music lovers, showcasing antique pianos from around the world.

Pro tip: Book ferry tickets in advance—this place gets crowded!

Afternoon: Zhongshan Road – A Food Lover’s Paradise

After returning from Gulangyu, head to Zhongshan Road, Xiamen’s most famous pedestrian street. It’s a mix of shopping, history, and street food.

Must-try snacks:
- Sha Cha Noodles (沙茶面): A savory peanut-based noodle soup.
- Oyster Omelette (蚵仔煎): Crispy on the outside, juicy on the inside.
- Peanut Soup (花生汤): A sweet, creamy local dessert.

Evening: Zeng Cuo An – Hipster Vibes by the Beach

Wrap up your day at Zeng Cuo An, a bohemian beachside village. It’s packed with quirky cafes, souvenir shops, and seafood stalls. Grab a coconut, stroll along the shore, and enjoy the sunset.

Day 2: Nanputuo Temple & Xiamen University

Morning: Nanputuo Temple – A Spiritual Retreat

Begin your second day at Nanputuo Temple, one of China’s most famous Buddhist temples. Nestled at the foot of Wulao Peak, it offers tranquility and stunning architecture.

  • Climb the Wulao Peak for a breathtaking view of the city.
  • Try the temple’s vegetarian lunch—simple yet delicious.

Afternoon: Xiamen University – The Most Beautiful Campus in China

Just a short walk from Nanputuo, Xiamen University (XMU) is worth a visit. Its lush gardens, colonial buildings, and lakes make it feel like a resort.

Don’t miss:
- Furong Tunnel: A student-decorated tunnel filled with graffiti and art.
- Lover’s Lake: A romantic spot with black swans gliding on the water.

Evening: Hulishan Fortress & Beach Walk

Head to Hulishan Fortress, a historic coastal defense site with a giant Krupp cannon. The nearby beach is perfect for an evening stroll.

For dinner, try Seafood Street near Heping Wharf—fresh catches cooked Fujian-style.

Day 3: Island Hopping & Relaxation

Morning: Wanshi Botanical Garden – A Green Escape

If you love nature, Wanshi Botanical Garden is a must. Highlights include:
- Cactus Zone: Featuring towering cacti and succulents.
- Rainforest Section: Misty pathways with exotic plants.

Afternoon: Island Hopping – Yuandang Lake & Jimei

  • Yuandang Lake: Rent a bike and cycle around this scenic lake.
  • Jimei District: Visit the Jimei School Village, a cultural landmark built by overseas Chinese philanthropist Tan Kah Kee.

Evening: Final Food Adventure at Shapowei

End your trip at Shapowei, an artsy district with murals, indie shops, and trendy eateries. Try:
- Taro Dumplings (芋包): A savory-sweet Fujian specialty.
- Seafood Hotpot: The perfect farewell meal.

Xiamen may be compact, but its mix of culture, nature, and cuisine ensures an unforgettable getaway. Pack light, bring your camera, and get ready to fall in love with this coastal gem!
