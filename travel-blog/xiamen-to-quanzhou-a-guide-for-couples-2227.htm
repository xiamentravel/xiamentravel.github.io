Xiamen to Quanzhou: A Guide for Couples

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Planning a romantic getaway from Xiamen to Quanzhou? This coastal route in Fujian Province offers a perfect blend of history, culture, and breathtaking scenery. Whether you're strolling through ancient streets, indulging in local delicacies, or soaking in the coastal vibes, this guide will help you and your partner make the most of your trip.

Why Xiamen to Quanzhou is Perfect for Couples

Scenic Coastal Drives

The journey between Xiamen and Quanzhou is dotted with picturesque coastal views. Rent a car or hire a private driver to enjoy the ride at your own pace, stopping at hidden beaches or cliffside viewpoints for impromptu photo sessions.

Rich Cultural Heritage

Quanzhou, a UNESCO-listed port city, is a treasure trove of history. From ancient temples to maritime museums, every corner tells a story. Couples who love exploring heritage sites will find plenty to admire.

Foodie Adventures

Fujian cuisine is a highlight of this trip. Share bowls of shaxian noodles, savor fresh seafood, or try the famous ou jian (oyster omelet) together. Food markets and night stalls add a fun, casual vibe to your dates.

Must-Visit Stops Along the Way

Gulangyu Island (Before Leaving Xiamen)

If you haven’t explored Gulangyu yet, spend a day here before heading to Quanzhou. This car-free island is ideal for couples:
- Piano Museum: A whimsical spot for music lovers.
- Sunlight Rock: Hike up for panoramic views and a romantic sunset.
- Hidden Cafés: Cozy spots perfect for quiet moments.

Anxi Tea Plantations

On the way to Quanzhou, detour to Anxi, the birthplace of Tieguanyin tea.
- Tea Tasting Sessions: Sip fragrant oolong together.
- Rolling Green Hills: Walk hand-in-hand through terraced fields.

Quanzhou’s Romantic Highlights

Kaiyuan Temple

This ancient Buddhist temple, with its iconic twin pagodas, is a serene escape. Light incense together and wander through the peaceful courtyards.

Tumen Street

A lively pedestrian street where you can:
- Try runbing (spring rolls) from street vendors.
- Browse quirky souvenir shops for keepsakes.
- Listen to traditional Nanyin music performances.

Qingjing Mosque

One of China’s oldest mosques, blending Arabic and Chinese architecture. The quiet ambiance makes it a reflective spot for couples.

Unique Date Ideas in Quanzhou

A Sunset at Chongwu Ancient City

Head to this lesser-known coastal town near Quanzhou for:
- Ancient Stone Walls: Perfect backdrop for photos.
- Empty Beaches: Enjoy private walks by the sea.

DIY Pottery in Dehua

Known as the "Porcelain Capital," Dehua offers workshops where you can craft pottery together—a fun and memorable souvenir.

Where to Stay: Romantic Accommodations

Xiamen: Beachfront Boutique Hotels

  • The Cozy Nest: A stylish B&B near Hulishan Fortress.
  • Gulangyu Vintage Inn: Heritage charm with sea views.

Quanzhou: Historic Guesthouses

  • 1916 Art Villa: A chic stay in a repurposed factory.
  • Quanzhou Ancient Courtyard Hotel: Traditional Fujian architecture with modern comforts.

Tips for Couples Traveling Together

  • Pack Light: Opt for comfy shoes and layers—Quanzhou’s weather can shift.
  • Learn Basic Phrases: While English is limited, locals appreciate efforts like "Xièxie" (Thank you).
  • Slow Down: Don’t rush. The best moments happen when you linger over tea or a view.

Now, go ahead and craft your own love story on the road from Xiamen to Quanzhou!
