Xiamen Hostels Near Popular Food Streets & Attractions

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Xiamen, a coastal gem in Fujian Province, is a dream destination for travelers who crave a mix of urban charm, island vibes, and mouthwatering street food. Whether you're backpacking solo or exploring with friends, staying in a hostel near Xiamen’s bustling food streets and top attractions is the ultimate way to immerse yourself in the city’s culture. Here’s your guide to the best hostels that put you steps away from the action.

Why Stay Near Food Streets & Attractions?

Xiamen’s food scene is legendary, and its attractions—from Gulangyu Island to Nanputuo Temple—are must-visits. Choosing a hostel in these lively areas means:

  • Convenience: No long commutes—just step outside and dive into the fun.
  • Local Flavors: Night markets like Zhongshan Road are a foodie’s paradise.
  • Social Vibes: Hostels in these spots attract fellow travelers, making it easy to meet new friends.

Top Hostels Near Xiamen’s Best Food Streets

1. Lazy Cat Hostel (Near Zhongshan Road)

Why Stay Here?
Zhongshan Road is Xiamen’s most famous food street, packed with stalls serving sha cha noodles, oyster omelets, and peanut soup. Lazy Cat Hostel is just a 5-minute walk away, blending comfort with a quirky, artsy vibe.

Highlights:
- Cozy dorm rooms with privacy curtains.
- Rooftop lounge with city views.
- Free walking tours to hidden food gems.

Nearby Eats:
- Huangzehe Peanut Soup: A century-old spot for sweet, creamy peanut soup.
- Bao’s Dumplings: Juicy xiaolongbao (soup dumplings) that melt in your mouth.

2. Gulangyu International Youth Hostel (Near Longtou Road)

Why Stay Here?
This hostel is a short ferry ride from Gulangyu Island’s iconic colonial architecture and a stone’s throw from Longtou Road, the island’s food hub.

Highlights:
- Colonial-era building with vintage charm.
- Bike rentals to explore the car-free island.
- On-site café serving local teas.

Nearby Eats:
- Zhang Sanfeng Milk Tea: A cult-favorite bubble tea spot.
- Ye’s Seafood: Fresh catches cooked Fujian-style.

3. Xiamen Loft Hostel (Near Shapowei Art Zone)

Why Stay Here?
Shapowei is Xiamen’s hipster haven, where street art meets seafood grills. This industrial-chic hostel is perfect for creative souls.

Highlights:
- Artsy décor with local murals.
- Shared kitchen for cooking with fellow travelers.
- Live music nights at nearby bars.

Nearby Eats:
- Ah Jie BBQ: Skewers grilled to perfection.
- Jiangjun Duck Rice: A spicy, fragrant must-try.

Hostels Close to Xiamen’s Top Attractions

1. SeaWoo Hostel (Near Nanputuo Temple)

Why Stay Here?
Nanputuo Temple is a serene escape with lush gardens and Buddhist heritage. SeaWoo offers tranquility while keeping you close to the city buzz.

Highlights:
- Zen-inspired common areas.
- Temple-visit itineraries provided.
- Vegan breakfast options.

Pro Tip: Hike the temple’s trails at sunrise for breathtaking views.

2. Xiamen Island Inn (Near Hulishan Fortress)

Why Stay Here?
Hulishan Fortress, with its historic cannons and coastal views, is a short stroll from this beachfront hostel.

Highlights:
- Ocean-view dorms.
- Bonfire nights on the beach.
- Kayak rentals for coastal adventures.

3. Garden Hostel Xiamen (Near Xiamen University)

Why Stay Here?
Xiamen University’s stunning campus and the nearby Baicheng Beach are major draws. This hostel feels like a tropical retreat.

Highlights:
- Lush garden courtyard.
- Free campus tours.
- Nightly seafood BBQs.

Budget Tips for Food & Fun

  • Street Food Savings: Dishes like tuo tuo bing (crispy pancakes) cost under $1.
  • Hostel Perks: Many offer free bike rentals or group discounts to attractions.
  • Off-Peak Visits: Avoid holidays for cheaper rates and shorter lines.

Final Thoughts

From slurping noodles at midnight markets to exploring Gulangyu’s piano-filled lanes, Xiamen is best experienced when you’re right in the heart of it all. These hostels aren’t just places to sleep—they’re gateways to unforgettable adventures. Pack light, bring your appetite, and get ready to fall in love with Xiamen!
