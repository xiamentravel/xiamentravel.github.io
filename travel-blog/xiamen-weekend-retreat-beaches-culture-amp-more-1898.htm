Xiamen Weekend Retreat: Beaches, Culture & More

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Xiamen, a coastal gem in Fujian Province, is the perfect destination for a weekend getaway. With its pristine beaches, rich history, and vibrant food scene, this city offers something for every traveler. Whether you’re looking to relax by the sea, explore ancient temples, or indulge in local delicacies, Xiamen won’t disappoint.

Why Xiamen Should Be Your Next Weekend Escape

Stunning Beaches for Every Mood

Xiamen boasts some of China’s most beautiful beaches, each with its own unique vibe.

Hulishan Beach – Known for its golden sands and clear waters, this spot is ideal for sunbathing and swimming. The nearby Hulishan Fortress adds a historical touch to your beach day.

Baicheng Beach – Located near Xiamen University, this beach is popular among students and locals. The soft sand and gentle waves make it perfect for a leisurely stroll at sunset.

Gulangyu Island’s Hidden Coves – While not technically a single beach, Gulangyu’s coastline is dotted with secluded spots where you can escape the crowds.

Cultural Gems You Can’t Miss

Xiamen’s history is as captivating as its scenery.

Nanputuo Temple

This ancient Buddhist temple, nestled at the foot of Wulao Peak, is a serene retreat from the city’s hustle. Climb to the top for panoramic views of Xiamen.

Gulangyu Island’s Colonial Architecture

A UNESCO World Heritage Site, Gulangyu is a car-free island filled with European-style villas and charming alleyways. Don’t miss the Piano Museum, showcasing rare instruments from around the world.

Zhongshan Road Pedestrian Street

A blend of old and new, this bustling street is lined with traditional Minnan-style buildings housing modern shops and eateries.

Foodie Adventures in Xiamen

No trip to Xiamen is complete without diving into its culinary scene.

Must-Try Local Dishes

  • Shacha Noodles (沙茶面) – A savory noodle soup with a peanut-based broth.
  • Oyster Omelette (蚵仔煎) – Crispy on the outside, juicy on the inside.
  • Taro Paste (芋泥) – A sweet dessert made from mashed taro and lard.

Best Places to Eat

  • Huangzehe Peanut Soup Shop – Famous for its peanut soup and traditional snacks.
  • Jiahe Seafood Restaurant – Fresh seafood dishes right by the harbor.

Off-the-Beaten-Path Experiences

For those who want to explore beyond the usual tourist spots:

Xiamen Botanical Garden – A lush oasis with exotic plants and tranquil ponds.
Jimei School Village – A unique area founded by philanthropist Tan Kah Kee, blending education and architecture.
Wanshi Botanical Garden’s Cable Car – Soar above the city for breathtaking views.

Practical Tips for Your Trip

  • Best Time to Visit: Spring (March-May) and autumn (September-November) offer pleasant weather.
  • Getting Around: Public buses and taxis are convenient, but renting a bike is a great way to explore.
  • Language Tips: While Mandarin is widely spoken, learning a few Minnan phrases can earn you smiles.

Xiamen’s mix of natural beauty, culture, and flavors makes it an unforgettable weekend destination. Pack your bags and get ready to fall in love with this coastal paradise!
