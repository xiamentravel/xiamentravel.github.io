The Best 5-Day Xiamen Itinerary for Food & Culture

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Xiamen, a coastal gem in Fujian Province, is a paradise for food lovers and culture enthusiasts. With its blend of colonial architecture, vibrant night markets, and stunning island scenery, this city offers an unforgettable travel experience. Here’s the ultimate 5-day itinerary to savor the best of Xiamen’s cuisine and heritage.

Day 1: Exploring Gulangyu Island & Local Delicacies

Morning: Ferry Ride to Gulangyu Island

Start your journey with a short ferry ride to Gulangyu, a car-free island known for its European-style buildings and piano museums. Stroll along Longtou Road, where you’ll find quaint cafes and souvenir shops. Don’t miss Shuzhuang Garden, a seaside villa with lush gardens and panoramic ocean views.

Lunch: Seafood Feast at Lianhua Road

Head back to Xiamen’s mainland for lunch at Lianhua Road, famous for its seafood restaurants. Try local specialties like oyster omelet (蚵仔煎, pronounced ô-á-jiān) and steamed fish with pickled vegetables. Pair your meal with a cold Tsingtao beer for the full experience.

Afternoon: Hulishan Fortress

Visit Hulishan Fortress, a historic coastal defense site with a massive Krupp cannon. The seaside walk offers breathtaking views of the Taiwan Strait.

Evening: Zhongshan Road Night Market

As the sun sets, dive into Zhongshan Road’s bustling night market. Sample street food like peanut soup (花生汤), fried taro balls (炸芋枣), and satay noodles (沙茶面). The mix of Fujianese and Taiwanese flavors here is irresistible.

Day 2: Nanputuo Temple & Xiamen University

Morning: Nanputuo Temple

Begin your day at Nanputuo Temple, a serene Buddhist complex nestled at the foot of Wulao Peak. Climb to the summit for a stunning view of Xiamen’s skyline.

Lunch: Vegetarian Feast at Nanputuo’s Restaurant

The temple’s vegetarian restaurant serves exquisite dishes like mushroom stew and lotus root soup. Even meat-lovers will appreciate the flavors.

Afternoon: Xiamen University & Furong Tunnel

Explore Xiamen University, one of China’s most beautiful campuses. Walk through Furong Tunnel, a student-decorated passageway covered in colorful graffiti.

Evening: Shapowei Art Zone

Unwind at Shapowei, a hip arts district with indie cafes and galleries. Try seaweed jelly (石花膏) from a street vendor while admiring the creative murals.

Day 3: Hakka Culture & Tulou Day Trip

Full-Day Excursion: Fujian Tulou

Take a guided tour to the UNESCO-listed Fujian Tulou, circular earthen buildings built by the Hakka people. Visit Chengqi Lou and Yuchang Lou, where you’ll learn about communal living traditions.

Lunch: Hakka Homestyle Cooking

Enjoy a rustic Hakka meal inside a Tulou, featuring dishes like braised pork with preserved vegetables (梅菜扣肉) and stuffed tofu (酿豆腐).

Evening: Relax at Zeng Cuo’an

Return to Xiamen and unwind at Zeng Cuo’an, a bohemian beachside village. Sip on sugar cane juice while browsing quirky handicraft stalls.

Day 4: Island Hopping & Coastal Cuisine

Morning: Wanshi Botanical Garden

Hike through Wanshi Botanical Garden’s tropical greenery, stopping at the iconic Stone Rock Temple.

Lunch: Fresh Seafood at Heping Wharf

Head to Heping Wharf for a seafood lunch. Must-tries include steamed crab with ginger and stir-fried clams with basil.

Afternoon: Island Hopping to Kinmen (Optional)

If time allows, take a ferry to Kinmen (Taiwan-controlled islands) for a unique cross-strait experience. Otherwise, relax at Xiamen’s Baicheng Beach.

Evening: Jimei District’s Night Bazaar

Explore Jimei’s lively night market, where grilled squid and bubble tea are crowd favorites.

Day 5: Tea Culture & Souvenir Shopping

Morning: Xiamen Tea Market

Visit a local tea market to sample Fujian’s famous Tieguanyin oolong tea. Learn brewing techniques from a tea master.

Lunch: Minnan Cuisine at Jiageng Restaurant

Savor Minnan dishes like Buddha Jumps Over the Wall (佛跳墙) and crispy oyster pancakes at this iconic eatery.

Afternoon: Shopping at SM Mall

Pick up souvenirs like tea sets, pineapple cakes, and lacquerware at Xiamen’s largest shopping complex.

Evening: Sunset at Huandao Road

End your trip with a bike ride along Huandao Road, Xiamen’s scenic coastal highway. Stop for a coconut drink and watch the sun dip below the horizon.

This 5-day itinerary ensures you’ll leave Xiamen with a full stomach and a heart full of memories. From historic Tulou to sizzling street food, every moment is a celebration of Fujian’s rich culture.
