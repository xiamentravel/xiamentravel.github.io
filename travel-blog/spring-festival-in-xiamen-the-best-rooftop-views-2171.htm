Spring Festival in Xiamen: The Best Rooftop Views

  • Home
  • Travel Blog
  • Blog Details

Xiamen, a coastal gem in Fujian Province, transforms into a dazzling spectacle during the Spring Festival. While the city’s lantern-lit streets and bustling markets are a feast for the senses, some of the most unforgettable moments can be found high above the ground. From sky bars to hidden terraces, Xiamen’s rooftops offer breathtaking views of fireworks, cityscapes, and the sea—making them the perfect spots to celebrate the Lunar New Year.

Why Rooftop Views Are a Must in Xiamen

Xiamen’s unique blend of modern skyscrapers and traditional Minnan architecture creates a skyline unlike any other. During Spring Festival, the city lights up in red and gold, with fireworks bursting over Gulangyu Island and the shimmering waters of the Taiwan Strait. A rooftop vantage point lets you take it all in without the crowds, offering a serene yet festive atmosphere.

The Best Time to Visit

The magic happens after sunset. Most rooftop venues open early for dinner service, but the real show begins around 8 PM when fireworks illuminate the sky. Arrive before dusk to secure a prime spot and enjoy the transition from daylight to a glittering nightscape.

Top Rooftop Spots for Spring Festival

1. The Rooftop Bar at The Langham, Xiamen

Perched on the 38th floor, this luxurious spot offers panoramic views of the city and the sea. During Spring Festival, they host special events with themed cocktails and live music. The open-air terrace is ideal for watching fireworks over Hulishan Fortress.

What to Expect:

  • Signature cocktails infused with local flavors like longan and osmanthus.
  • A curated menu of Fujian-inspired tapas.
  • Cozy fire pits for chilly evenings.

2. Sky High at Ascott Xiamen

This sleek rooftop lounge is a favorite among locals for its unobstructed views of Yundang Lake. During the holiday, the space is adorned with traditional Chinese decorations, creating a festive yet modern ambiance.

Highlights:

  • A dedicated "fireworks viewing" package with champagne.
  • Live DJ sets blending electronic beats with traditional Chinese melodies.
  • Private cabanas for intimate gatherings.

3. Secret Garden Rooftop Café

Tucked away in Siming District, this hidden gem feels like a private escape. The café’s lush greenery and fairy lights make it a romantic spot to ring in the New Year.

Why It’s Special:

  • Homemade tangyuan (sweet glutinous rice balls) served as a Lunar New Year treat.
  • A small-batch tea selection featuring Tieguanyin from nearby Anxi.
  • Limited seating, so reservations are a must.

Insider Tips for the Ultimate Experience

Book Early

Rooftop venues fill up fast during Spring Festival. Make reservations at least two weeks in advance, especially for popular spots like The Langham.

Dress for the Weather

Xiamen’s winter evenings can be breezy. Bring a stylish jacket or opt for a venue with heat lamps.

Capture the Moment

Bring a camera with night-mode capabilities. The contrast of fireworks against Xiamen’s skyline is a photographer’s dream.

Beyond the Rooftops: Nearby Festive Activities

While the rooftops are a highlight, don’t miss these Spring Festival experiences:
- Nanputuo Temple: Join locals for midnight prayers and incense lighting.
- Zhongshan Road Night Market: Sample street food like peanut soup and oyster omelets.
- Gulangyu Island: Take a night ferry to see the historic buildings lit up in holiday colors.

Final Thoughts

Xiamen’s rooftops offer a unique way to celebrate Spring Festival—combining luxury, tradition, and unbeatable views. Whether you’re sipping cocktails under the stars or sharing tangyuan with loved ones, these elevated spots promise a memorable start to the Year of the Dragon.
